PHASE 9

Diabetes & Metabolic Health

GLUCOSE · WEIGHT · MEDICATIONS · LIFESTYLE · NON-DIAGNOSTIC

Phase 9 is the Diabetes & Metabolic Health brain of Elisence. It takes glucose readings, weight trends, medication events and lifestyle signals and turns them into safe, non-diagnostic intelligence that helps people, families and clinicians understand patterns without replacing a doctor.

  • Built on structured events, not screenshots or free text.
  • Understands GLP-1, insulin and oral diabetes medications via Phase 6.
  • Five-language ready: EN, FA, AR, TR, RO.

1. What Phase 9 Really Is

Phase 9 is a structured, ethical Diabetes & Metabolic Health layer. It does not diagnose diabetes, choose treatments or change doses. Instead, it organises what is already happening: glucose readings, weight, medications, food, movement and sleep.

The engine turns this stream of events into timelines, trends and gentle insights that a user – or their clinician – can understand at a glance. It gives context to questions such as:

  • “What happens to my glucose when I take my GLP-1 on time?”
  • “How does my night-time snack affect my morning readings?”
  • “What changed in the weeks when my weight started to move?”

All of this is done inside a non-diagnostic perimeter, with clear reminders that medical decisions belong to qualified professionals.

2. Core Engine & Event Model

Phase 9 is implemented as a set of well-defined events with explicit schemas:

2.1 Glucose Events

  • Fasting, pre-meal, post-meal and bedtime readings.
  • Manual entry or device-imported values (future integrations).
  • Tags for “sick day”, “travel”, “stress” to explain outliers.

2.2 Weight & Body Events

  • Daily or weekly weigh-ins, linked to Phase 3 weight engine.
  • Optional waist or body-composition entries (wellness posture).
  • Flags for rapid changes that should be discussed with a clinician.

2.3 Medication Events

  • Structured medication actions from Phase 6 (take / miss / stop / change).
  • Support for GLP-1, insulin, Metformin and other core diabetes drugs.
  • Zero-Trust parsing: no guessing of drugs or doses.

2.4 Lifestyle Events

  • Food windows (not calorie counting), movement, sleep quality, stress tags.
  • Simple, human language — “good walk”, “heavy dinner”, “no sleep”.

Every event has timestamps, provenance and audit fields so trends can be trusted by clinicians and ministries later.

3. Pattern & Insight Layer (Non-Diagnostic)

On top of the raw events, Phase 9 calculates safe, explainable patterns:

3.1 Daily & Weekly Views

  • Simple charts of glucose vs time-of-day and meals.
  • Weight trend lines with gentle commentary (“steady”, “slow change”).
  • Overview of medication adherence without blame or shame.

3.2 Pattern Highlights

  • “Many high readings after late meals” – suggesting a conversation with a clinician.
  • “More stable readings on weeks with regular walks.”
  • “Large gaps in logging – would you like a reminder schedule?”

3.3 Safety Language

  • No verdicts like “controlled / uncontrolled”.
  • Instead: “Your data shows X; please discuss options with your doctor.”
  • Emergency tones (very high / very low values) always push to urgent care.

4. Integration With Other Phases

Phase 9 does not live alone – it is deeply linked to the rest of Elisence:

4.1 Phase 3 – Care & Weight-Loss

  • Shares weight, movement and check-in events.
  • Care teams can see both weight-loss and glucose journeys together.

4.2 Phase 6 – Global Medication Brain

  • Uses the medication engine to understand GLP-1, insulin and oral drugs.
  • Supports safe, multilingual capture of medication behaviour.

4.3 Phase 7 & Phase 8

  • Women+ engine can see diabetes-related patterns around cycles (non-diagnostic).
  • AHE (Phase 8) receives anonymised aggregates for public-health insights.

5. Safety, Regulation & Clear Limits

Phase 9 is deliberately designed inside a safe regulatory boundary:

  • NHS / DTAC style: health IT component, not a triage tool.
  • FDA CDS: non-device clinical decision support – user can see the basis of every output.
  • EU MDR: wellness / information posture, not an automatic treatment system.
  • GCC / PDPL: ready for regional data-residency and privacy rules.

Clear limits:

  • No diagnosis of diabetes or complications.
  • No adjustment of insulin or GLP-1 doses.
  • No replacement for endocrinologists, GPs or emergency services.

The engine explains patterns and organises reality; decisions stay with humans.

6. One-Sentence Summary

Phase 9 is the Diabetes & Metabolic Health brain of Elisence – a multilingual, non-diagnostic engine that organises glucose, weight, medication and lifestyle signals into safe, audit-ready patterns for people, clinicians and ministries around the world.
