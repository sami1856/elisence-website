PHASE 9

Diabetes & Metabolic Health Engine

Phase 9 is the Diabetes & Metabolic Health brain of Elisence – a governed, non-diagnostic engine that understands glucose, weight, GLP-1 journeys and metabolic risk, and turns them into safe, structured, multilingual intelligence for citizens, clinicians and ministries.

Built for GLP-1 and diabetes at scale – Zero-Trust, multi-language, regulator-ready, and fully integrated with the rest of Elisence.

1. What Phase 9 Is

Phase 9 is the Diabetes & Metabolic Health Engine of Elisence. It focuses on:

  • Type 2 diabetes and pre-diabetes.
  • GLP-1–based weight-loss programmes and obesity management.
  • Metabolic risk for citizens using medication, lifestyle changes or both.

It is:

  • Non-diagnostic by design – it never replaces a doctor or prescribes treatment.
  • Risk-oriented and education-oriented – built to support safe decisions, not make them.
  • Multi-language – designed from day one for EN, FA, AR, TR, RO.
  • Globally compliant – aligned with NHS, FDA, EU MDR and GCC/PDPL expectations.

In one line: Phase 9 turns fragmented diabetes and metabolic data into safe, explainable intelligence that supports citizens, clinicians and ministries without becoming a diagnostic device.

2. Technical Core — How Phase 9 Works

The Phase 9 engine brings together multiple data sources and transforms them into structured risk views.

2.1 Inputs (Signals into the Engine)

  • Blood glucose logs (fingerstick, CGM, manual entries).
  • HbA1c values and other lab markers (via Phase 8 – Blood Core).
  • Weight curves and BMI trends (linked to Phase 3 – Weight & Care).
  • Medication data (GLP-1, insulin, oral agents) via Phase 6 – Global Medication Brain.
  • Lifestyle signals: activity, steps, sleep, nutrition patterns.
  • Symptoms and PRO: hypo/hyper feelings, dizziness, thirst, fatigue.

2.2 Core Diabetes & GLP-1 Logic

  • Risk tagging: tags for hypo-risk, hyper-risk, volatility, poor adherence, fast weight change, etc.
  • Threshold ladders: jurisdiction-aware ranges for glucose, A1c and weight dynamics.
  • Trend detection: improving, stable, or deteriorating metabolic patterns.
  • Zero-Trust checks: low-quality or conflicting data is flagged, not silently accepted.

2.3 Outputs (What the Engine Produces)

  • Structured risk snapshots for each user (non-diagnostic, explainable).
  • Safe textual explanations in five languages, adapted to role and country.
  • Flags for Elisa (Phase 5) to speak about risk in a calm, supportive tone.
  • Aggregated analytics for clinics and ministries (when enabled) via Phase 8.

3. Safety & Regulatory Posture

Phase 9 is designed from the start to be safe for citizens and comfortable for regulators.

3.1 Dual-Mode Design

  • Wellness / Education Mode (default)
    • Always ON and ready to deploy to the public.
    • Provides education, lifestyle guidance and gentle risk awareness.
    • No diagnosis, no treatment decisions, no numerical risk promises.
  • Medical-Adjacent Mode (restricted)
    • Provides tighter thresholds, structured risk views and dashboards.
    • OFF by default and only enabled by ministries or accredited clinical partners.
    • Controlled through feature flags, environment variables and governance.

3.2 Alignment with Major Frameworks

  • NHS – aligned with diabetes self-management and safety messaging guidelines.
  • FDA Non-Device CDS – users can understand the basis of outputs; no opaque decisions.
  • EU MDR – stays outside direct diagnosis/prescription; built as decision support and education.
  • GCC / PDPL – supports data residency, privacy and public-health analytics boundaries.

3.3 Zero-Trust & BBVP

  • Zero-Trust: no guessing of values; uncertainty is surfaced and users are asked for clarification.
  • BBVP (Build-Back-Verify-Prove): each engine and threshold must be tested and evidenced before being claimed as DONE.

4. Data Structures & Evidence Layer

Phase 9 uses clearly defined schemas that make every decision traceable and auditable.

  • diabetes_profiles – long-term context: diagnosis status, type, duration, comorbidities.
  • glucose_events – timestamped readings with source, units and confidence.
  • medication_intakes – linked to Phase 6 with dose, timing and confidence flags.
  • lifestyle_events – activity, sleep, nutrition, stress markers.
  • risk_snapshots – frozen views of risk tags, thresholds and explanations at each evaluation.
  • audit_logs – WORM-style logs for regulators and internal QA.

This structure allows a regulator or ministry to see exactly how a risk message was generated, which signals were used, and which thresholds were applied.

5. Integration with Other Phases

Phase 9 is deeply connected to the rest of Elisence:

5.1 Phase 4 — Governance & Analytics

  • Diabetes risk results flow into comparative intelligence and RWE analytics.
  • WORM ledger and provenance ensure traceable exports and dashboards.

5.2 Phase 5 — Elisa Personality & Dopamine

  • Elisa uses Phase 9 signals to coach users kindly about risk, adherence and lifestyle.

5.3 Phase 6 — Global Medication Brain

  • Medication understanding (names, doses, timing) is delegated to Phase 6.
  • Phase 9 uses structured medication events to reason about GLP-1 and diabetes regimens.

5.4 Phase 7 — Women+ & Phase 8 — AHE

  • Women+ pathways include metabolic and pregnancy-related diabetes, feeding back into Phase 9 rules.
  • AHE Core-2 (blood) and Core-3 (diabetes) reuse the Phase 9 logic for national dashboards and risk engines.

5.5 Phase 13 — Voice / Zero-Typing

  • Citizens can speak naturally (“Elisa, my sugar was 210 after dinner”).
  • Speech is transcribed, interpreted by Phase 6 & 9, then turned into safe, voice-ready explanations.

6. How Phase 9 Is Used in Real Life

6.1 Citizens

  • Track glucose, weight and medications with minimal friction (including voice).
  • Receive gentle, multilingual explanations about patterns: “improving”, “stable”, “needs attention”.
  • Get nudges to move, hydrate, sleep better and speak to clinicians earlier.

6.2 Women+ Users

  • Women using GLP-1 or at risk of gestational diabetes get integrated support via Phases 7 and 9.
  • Signals are handled with extra caution and clear non-diagnostic language.

6.3 Clinicians & Clinics (when enabled)

  • View structured risk summaries, trend charts and adherence patterns for their own patients.
  • Use Elisence as a time-saving intake and context layer, not as an automatic decision-maker.

6.4 Ministries & Public-Health Teams

  • Monitor anonymised GLP-1 adoption, adherence and metabolic trends at population level.
  • Evaluate impact of national programmes (education, medication access, lifestyle campaigns).

7. Investor & Regulator Questions

Liability — “Are you acting like a virtual doctor?”

Answer: No. Phase 9 never diagnoses or prescribes. It provides structured risk awareness and educational guidance, with clear “this is not a diagnosis” posture in every jurisdiction.

Safety — “What about hypo/hyper-glycaemia risk?”

Answer: Potentially dangerous patterns trigger strong escalation messages (“seek urgent medical help”, “contact your clinician”), not home treatment instructions or dose changes.

Data Quality & Bias — “Can we trust the numbers?”

Answer: All data goes through Zero-Trust cleaning; low-quality or conflicting data is flagged, and risk outputs clearly indicate uncertainty. Thresholds are calibrated to avoid dangerous false reassurance.

Scalability — “Can this handle national-scale data?”

Answer: Phase 9 is designed for pre-computation of aggregates, efficient querying and horizontal scaling, so ministries can monitor millions of events per day.

Jurisdiction Switching — “How do you handle UK vs EU vs GCC?”

Answer: The engine reads jurisdiction from Passport (country, system) and applies appropriate ranges, wording and policy boundaries per deployment.

Voice — “Is voice safe in multiple languages?”

Answer: Voice (Phase 13) is governed by the same safety rules. Low ASR confidence or unclear phrases lead to clarification prompts, not silent assumptions.
