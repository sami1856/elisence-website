PHASE 9

Diabetes & Metabolic Health Intelligence

GLUCOSE · WEIGHT · MEDICATION · LIFESTYLE · SAFE & NON-DIAGNOSTIC

Phase 9 is the Diabetes & Metabolic Health brain of Elisence. It takes glucose readings, weight trends, GLP-1 and other medications, food patterns, sleep and movement, and turns them into non-diagnostic, audit-ready metabolic insights that support citizens, clinicians and ministries – without ever replacing a doctor or a diabetes team.

  • Designed around real life with diabetes, not just lab numbers.
  • Non-diagnostic, guideline-aware patterns instead of hard medical decisions.
  • Five-language foundation: EN, FA, AR, TR, RO from day one.

1. What Phase 9 Really Is

Phase 9 is Elisence’s structured Diabetes & Metabolic Health Intelligence layer. It does not diagnose or prescribe. Instead, it organises scattered signals – glucose, weight, medication, meals, activity, sleep, stress – into patterns that are easy to understand, share and review.

The goal is clear: give people living with diabetes and metabolic risk a safe companion that helps them see trends, understand their own body and have better conversations with clinicians, while strict rules keep every output inside a non-diagnostic, wellness-aligned boundary.

Phase 9 is built to support:

  • People with type 2 diabetes or pre-diabetes using lifestyle and medication to stay in range.
  • GLP-1 and other weight-loss medication users who need structured tracking and safety nudges.
  • Citizens at metabolic risk (weight, blood pressure, family history) who want prevention and coaching.
  • Clinicians and clinics that need clean, structured data instead of chaotic screenshots and logs.
  • Ministries and insurers that want anonymised, k-anonymous insight into real-world metabolic health.

2. Core Engine & Data Model

Under the surface, Phase 9 runs a structured engine that keeps every data point tagged, traceable and audit-ready.

2.1 Metabolic Timeline Engine

  • Unified timeline for glucose readings (fingerstick, CGM summaries), weight and waist measurements.
  • Meal and snack events (time, type, estimated carb load, context tags like “eating out” or “late night”).
  • Activity events (walks, cardio, strength, daily movement score) and sleep quality markers.

2.2 Medication & GLP-1 Layer

  • Structured logging of GLP-1, insulin, metformin and other diabetes/metabolic medications.
  • Dose, timing, frequency and side-effect notes captured in a consistent schema.
  • Safe correlations: “this pattern often appears after dose day”, never “you must change dose”.

2.3 Risk & Pattern Tags

  • Non-diagnostic pattern tags like “frequent highs after dinner”, “weight trending down slowly”, “low activity weeks”.
  • Each tag linked to transparent rules that can be reviewed by clinicians and ministries.
  • Support for comorbid patterns: blood pressure, sleep Apnoea risk, metabolic syndrome risk profile.

All data structures are designed from the start for evidence packs, WORM audit and safe integration with national diabetes programmes.

3. People & Clinical Journeys Supported

Phase 9 is organised by real journeys, not only diagnoses.

3.1 Everyday Type 2 Diabetes Life

  • Daily overview of glucose ranges, meals and movement presented in simple colour-coded cards.
  • Gentle nudges: “many of your highs this week are after late dinners” instead of judgemental tone.
  • Support for shift workers, irregular schedules and real-world stress patterns.

3.2 GLP-1 & Weight-Loss Journeys

  • Tracking of weight, waist, appetite, side-effects and energy across weeks and months.
  • Context cards linking movement, sleep and weight change without promising magic results.
  • Safe reminders to speak with clinicians before stopping or changing any medication.

3.3 Prevention & High-Risk Populations

  • For pre-diabetes and metabolic risk, focus on lifestyle signals: food, movement, sleep, stress.
  • Non-stigmatising language around weight and body image; no crash-diet messaging.
  • Tools that can be rolled out via ministries, schools, workplaces and community programmes.

4. Safety, Ethics & Non-Diagnostic Boundaries

Diabetes and weight are emotionally heavy topics. Phase 9 is designed to be honest, kind and clinically responsible about what it can and cannot do.

4.1 Non-Diagnostic by Design

  • No statements like “you have diabetes”, “this is remission” or “your treatment is wrong”.
  • Instead: “your pattern looks like…, here are questions to discuss with your healthcare team”.
  • No insulin dose changes, no medication titration and no standalone treatment plans.

4.2 Emergency & Red-Flag Behaviour

  • Critical signs (e.g. symptoms of severe hypo/hyperglycaemia, chest pain, breathing difficulty) trigger clear “emergency help” messages.
  • For mental health red flags (burnout, hopelessness, self-harm thoughts), Phase 9 pushes towards crisis services and trusted support, never self-management tips.
  • No dieting pressure, no shame language and no competitive leaderboards.

4.3 Privacy, Weight & Stigma

  • Weight and glucose data are treated as highly sensitive; sharing is always explicit and reversible.
  • Copy avoids moral judgements about food, body size or “willpower”.
  • Focus is on sustainable habits, long-term health and small wins, not perfection.

5. Data Sources & Integration With Other Phases

Phase 9 connects the metabolic picture with the rest of Elisence.

5.1 Inputs & Devices

  • Manual logging for glucose, meals, activity and weight for low-resource settings.
  • Integration-ready schema for CGM summaries, smart scales and wearables.
  • Support for offline use with later sync when connectivity returns.

5.2 With Phase 3 (Care & Weight-Loss)

  • Shared GLP-1 and weight-loss structures; Phase 3 focuses on lifestyle coaching, Phase 9 on metabolic risk and patterning.
  • Unified journey for citizens taking GLP-1 for both diabetes and obesity.

5.3 With Phase 6 (Global Medication Intelligence)

  • Medication knowledge from Phase 6 informs safety messages around combinations, timing and adherence.
  • Clear separation: Phase 6 knows the drugs; Phase 9 organises the real-world behaviour around them.

5.4 With Phase 8 (AHE) & Ministries

  • Anonymised, k-anonymous feeds into AHE for population-level metabolic insights.
  • Enables dashboards such as “regions with highest out-of-range patterns” without exposing identities.
  • Supports national programmes for prevention, GLP-1 funding and hospital readmission reduction.

6. Why Phase 9 Matters for Investors & Ministries

Diabetes and metabolic disease are among the biggest, most expensive health challenges in the world. Phase 9 is designed as a strategic engine for tackling that challenge safely.

6.1 Scale of the Problem

  • Hundreds of millions living with diabetes, many more at metabolic risk.
  • Massive costs from complications, hospitalisations and loss of productivity.
  • Fragmented tools, low adherence and poor visibility for ministries.

6.2 Differentiation vs Generic Diabetes Apps

  • Most consumer apps focus on graphs and ads; Elisence focuses on governance and real outcomes.
  • Built to be ministry-grade: evidence packs, audit logs, zero-trust security and multi-language support.
  • Designed to plug into public programmes, not compete with them.

6.3 Policy & Programme Support

  • Supports value-based care models, GLP-1 reimbursement strategies and prevention campaigns.
  • Provides anonymised data that can guide funding, education and infrastructure planning.
  • Helps ministries show measurable impact to governments, donors and the public.

7. Limitations & Scope Boundaries

To stay safe and globally deployable, Phase 9 deliberately keeps clear boundaries:

  • Does not diagnose diabetes, remission or complications.
  • Does not adjust insulin, GLP-1 or any other medication doses.
  • Does not replace endocrinologists, diabetes nurses, dietitians or GPs.
  • Does not act as emergency triage or crisis counselling by itself.
  • Does not sell or share identifiable data with advertisers or data brokers.

These limits protect citizens, protect clinicians and make it easier for ministries to adopt Elisence as a trusted, compliant platform.
