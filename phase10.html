PHASE 10

User Profiles, Social Presence & Secure Attachments

VITRINE · COMMUNITY · ATTACHMENTS · TRUST

Phase 10 is the public face and social heart of Elisence. It turns citizens, families, clinicians, hospitals and ministries from anonymous data rows into living profiles, conversations and communities — inside a strict Zero-Trust, evidence-ready architecture.

  • Health-centric profiles and feed — built for care, not for clicks.
  • Secure Attachments Vault for reports, scans and legal documents.
  • Safer social graph that connects all other Elisence phases in one vitrine.

1. What Phase 10 Really Is

10.1 Core Definition

Phase 10 is Elisence’s User Profile & Social Identity Layer. It provides:

  • Rich personal profiles for citizens and families.
  • Verified professional profiles for doctors, nurses, clinics, pharmacists and partners.
  • Social presence: posts, reactions, shared achievements and topic communities.
  • A secure Attachments & Media Vault for documents, scans, reports and legal files.
  • Discovery and interaction that are safer than mainstream social networks, because they are built for health — not for engagement metrics.

10.2 Design Principles

  • Zero-Trust by design — every identity, file and action is verified and auditable.
  • BBVP (Build • Back • Verify • Prove) for every feature.
  • NHS / GDPR / EU MDR / HIPAA / GCC PDPL aware; health-data first, not ad-tech.
  • Non-diagnostic, non-prescriptive — no direct medical advice in feeds or comments.
  • Five-language foundation from day one (EN / FA / AR / TR / RO).

2. Citizen & Family Profiles

2.1 Personal Identity

Phase 10 gives every user a safe, modern health identity. A profile can include a photo or avatar, a short bio and key health focuses such as weight-loss, diabetes support, Women+ health or mental wellbeing. Every field is under the user’s control.

Privacy levels are simple and explicit: private, friends-only or public. Nothing becomes public by accident.

2.2 Family & Care Profiles

Parents and carers can manage profiles for children or elderly relatives, linked to Elisence Care modules. All sharing is consent-based and traceable. Sensitive details never appear in public views, only in clinical or family contexts where they belong.

2.3 Healthy Social Feed (Not Addictive)

Users can post progress updates, celebrate milestones from their weight-loss, diabetes or Women+ journeys, and share positive achievements powered by the Healthy Dopamine engine. The feed is designed not to be addictive:

  • No casino-style loops, endless scroll traps or manipulative notifications.
  • No weight-shaming or comparison leaderboards.
  • Only reinforcement of healthy, sustainable behaviours and rest days.

3. Professional & Organisation Profiles

3.1 Verified Professional Identity

Phase 10 allows clinicians, dietitians, psychologists, pharmacists, clinics and hospitals to hold verified professional profiles. Verification is tied to Elisence Passport V1 and the AHE (Phase 8), with country-specific rules for who can be approved.

Professional profiles can show credentials, specialities, affiliations, opening hours and educational posts. Ministries and regulators define:

  • who can be verified,
  • what professional accounts are allowed to publish,
  • which regions and programmes are enabled first.

The result is a trustable health network, not a generic, unmoderated forum.

4. Secure Attachments & Media Vault

4.1 Health Documents in One Safe Place

The Attachments & Media Vault is a core innovation of Phase 10. Users can store and share:

  • lab results, imaging, clinic and hospital letters,
  • prescription photos and medication summaries,
  • legal and consent documents,
  • photos and videos relevant to their health journeys.

Access is fully user-controlled: they decide who can see each file, for how long and for what purpose. Time-limited secure links, revocation controls and WORM audit logs turn the vault into the safest place to keep and share health documents.

Elisence does not rely on random public-cloud links. The entire flow is handled inside Elisence infrastructure, with healthcare-grade security and clear jurisdiction rules.

5. Safer Social Interaction

5.1 Built for Health, Not for Clicks

Phase 10 includes familiar social features — posts, comments, reactions and topic spaces — but rebuilt specifically for health:

  • clear content rules and human + AI moderation,
  • no tolerance for abuse, hate, bullying or organised misinformation campaigns,
  • reporting, escalation and bans for harmful behaviour.

Social interactions happen in a container designed for care and safety, not for ad impressions or engagement KPIs.

6. Voice, Accessibility & Languages

6.1 Voice-First Social Experience

With Phase 13 (Voice Engine), users can browse profiles, open posts, listen to updates and send short messages entirely by voice. Elisa can read key updates aloud and help visually impaired users navigate the social layer safely.

6.2 Accessibility & Blind-Assist

Phase 10 integrates with the Blind/Accessibility modules: high-contrast UI, voice-first flows and camera-assisted descriptions allow visually impaired users to participate fully in the Elisence community.

6.3 Five-Language Foundation

All profile labels, system messages and structural text are designed to support five languages from day one: English, Farsi, Arabic, Turkish and Romanian, with a clear path to add more languages in future phases.

7. Integration With Other Phases – The Vitrine of Elisence

Phase 10 is where the rest of Elisence becomes visible and human:

  • Phase 3 & 9: weight, GLP-1 and diabetes milestones can become shareable achievements (if the user chooses).
  • Phase 5: Healthy Dopamine events appear as subtle, ethical rewards in the feed.
  • Phase 6: structured medication behaviour drives safe, anonymised badges and gentle streaks.
  • Phase 7: Women+ journeys connect to respectful, trauma-aware communities.
  • Phase 8: professional and institutional identities are anchored via Passport V1 and the AHE engine.

In practice, Phase 10 is the vitrine where citizens, families, clinicians and ministries see the living side of Elisence.

8. Regulatory & Government Posture

8.1 Safe by Default

Citizen-facing flows are strictly non-diagnostic and non-prescriptive. Medical-adjacent features (professional dashboards, clinical pathways, public programmes) are guarded behind ON/OFF feature flags and can only be enabled by ministries or accredited health systems.

Phase 10 is designed to align with:

  • NHS DTAC / DCB0129–0160 principles.
  • GDPR / UK DPA privacy frameworks.
  • EU MDR boundaries for non-device / wellness software.
  • HIPAA-style expectations for health data handling.
  • GCC / Qatar / Saudi PDPL data-residency and privacy rules.

8.2 BBVP & WORM Audit

Every critical action — verification, attachment-sharing, role changes — can be logged to WORM audit trails. BBVP is applied to each module so that new features are always shipped with tests and evidence packs, not just promises.

9. Why Phase 10 Matters

For users, Phase 10 is where Elisence feels alive: a place to belong, to be seen, to receive support and to share progress safely.

For clinicians and hospitals, Phase 10 is a trusted social–clinical bridge, combining professional identity, secure documents and patient-friendly communication under clear, auditable rules.

For ministries and investors, Phase 10 is a strategic moat: it owns the health-centric social graph, provides a safe alternative to generic social networks for health discussions, and binds all other Elisence phases into one coherent public-facing experience.

10. One-Sentence Summary

Phase 10 turns Elisence from a powerful health engine into a trusted, human social universe — where profiles, conversations, achievements and secure documents live together under one globally compliant, Zero-Trust architecture.
