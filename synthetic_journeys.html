Synthetic Journeys & Demo Policy

The Elisence demo environment uses synthetic journeys: realistic but fully scripted examples that show how the platform behaves – without using any real patient or citizen data.

This page explains how synthetic journeys are designed, why they exist, and how they connect to the Zero-Trust and Non-Diagnostic policies for ministries, investors and partners.

1. What Is a Synthetic Journey?

A synthetic journey is a pre-scripted, fictional scenario that shows how Elisence handles signals, questions and events – without belonging to any real person.

  • Names, ages, locations and events are invented and not tied to real individuals.
  • Signals (for example: glucose curves, symptom patterns, sleep changes) are generated or anonymised examples.
  • Safety and non-diagnostic rules are fully active, exactly as in the real platform.
  • Journeys can be replayed and inspected by ministries and investors without privacy risk.

This allows reviewers to see the real behaviour of the engine with zero exposure of personal data.

2. Why Synthetic Journeys Matter

For national health systems, it is not enough to see slides or read documents – they need to see how the system behaves under different conditions. Synthetic journeys offer:

  • Safety: no citizen or patient is ever exposed in a public or semi-public demo.
  • Repeatability: the same scenario can be replayed by different reviewers.
  • Transparency: ministries can compare the textual guidance, probabilities and safety messages.
  • Auditability: outputs can be matched with engine rules and WORM logs for evidence.

This is especially important for Qatar, GCC and EU regulators who must see realistic behaviour without using real clinical data.

3. Example – Women+ Synthetic Scenarios

In the Women+ Engine demo, four synthetic journeys are used to show how Elisence responds across different life contexts and safety levels (for example: general, attention, emergency-advice-to-call-services).

  • Cycle changes and pain patterns across a typical month.
  • Scenario with stress, poor sleep and emotional pressure.
  • Scenario with red-flag symptoms where emergency guidance is needed.
  • Scenario with pregnancy-mode questions (with features kept OFF by default in real deployments).

Each journey is clearly marked as synthetic and is protected behind Zero-Trust access.

4. Example – Diabetes & Metabolic Journeys

For the Diabetes and Metabolic engines, synthetic journeys can demonstrate:

  • Changing glucose curves over several weeks.
  • Periods of good stability and periods of instability.
  • Educational messages about lifestyle, patterns and when to seek advice.
  • How probability-based insights are presented in non-diagnostic language.

Again, no real blood results, sensors or personal identifiers are used in the demo environment.

5. Synthetic vs Real Data – Clear Separation

Elisence keeps a strict separation between synthetic demo data and any real-world deployments.

  • Synthetic Used in the Cloudflare-protected demo and public presentations. No real identifiers, no real clinical records.
  • Real Used only in production deployments, inside-country or approved regions, under ministry or institutional control.

Demo traffic and real traffic are not mixed, and synthetic journeys do not leak into production environments.

6. Zero-Trust & Access Control for the Demo

The synthetic demo is protected by the same Zero-Trust principles as the main platform:

  • Access can require an email invite or one-time code.
  • Only approved reviewers (for example: ministry staff, investors, partners) can enter.
  • Traffic is monitored and can be cut off at any time.
  • No raw logs or engine internals are exposed through the browser.

This means Elisence can safely demonstrate complex women’s health and diabetes journeys for Qatar, GCC and international partners without opening the system to the general public.

7. How Ministries and Investors Use Synthetic Journeys

For ministries of health, investors and strategic partners, synthetic journeys act as a live storyboard of the platform:

  • Reviewers can walk through each step, reading the same messages a citizen would see.
  • They can compare different journeys (for example: low-risk vs high-risk patterns).
  • They can request specific “what if” scenarios to be scripted for their population needs.
  • They can verify that non-diagnostic and safety rules behave as promised in the Evidence Pack.

This closes the loop between documents, code and realistic behaviour on screen.

8. Connection to the Evidence Pack

Every synthetic journey is backed by real engine logic, feature flags and tests documented in the Evidence Hub. Ministries and partners can:

  • Open the Evidence Hub to see which engines and tests are running behind a demo.
  • Request WORM audit excerpts that show how the demo events were logged.
  • Access SEAL repositories for Women+, Diabetes and other phases under controlled conditions.

9. Summary

Synthetic journeys allow Elisence to show its full capability in Women+, Diabetes, Nutrition, Family and future phases – while remaining completely safe, private and aligned with Zero-Trust and Non-Diagnostic rules.

For access to the protected demo environment, ministries, investors and partners can contact the Elisence team to receive an invite under Cloudflare Zero-Trust.
