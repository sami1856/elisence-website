Phase 17 — Mental Health & Meditation Intelligence (MHME)

Mental Health & Meditation Intelligence Engine (MHME)

Phase 17 is Elisence’s mental health and meditation engine. It brings calm, grounding and emotional insight into the same regulated environment as diabetes, nutrition, women’s health and family care — without becoming an AI therapist or a diagnostic tool.

Core Definition

What Phase 17 Is

MHME is a safe emotional wellness layer inside Elisence. It provides stress support, breathing and grounding tools, short meditations and mood-awareness journeys in five languages, connected to the rest of the health OS:

  • Weight, sleep and lifestyle (Phase 3 & 5)
  • Women+ hormonal and life stages (Phase 7)
  • Diabetes & metabolic health (Phase 9)
  • Nutrition and fasting patterns (Phase 16)
  • Family and social context (Phase 10 & 15)

It is designed as a supportive companion, not a replacement for psychiatrists, psychologists or counsellors.

Design Anchors

Non-diagnostic, non-therapeutic Zero-Trust & Mental Vault NHS / MDR / FDA / GCC-aware 5 languages (EN / FA / AR / TR / RO) Trauma-aware, youth-safe tone

Stakeholder View

Who MHME Serves

  • Citizens under stress, poor sleep or mild anxiety
  • Women in cycle, pregnancy, postpartum or menopause
  • Teens and students facing exams or social pressure
  • Patients living with chronic disease burden
  • Governments building population-level wellbeing programmes

Safety Envelope

Wellness & emotional support only Helpline & clinician escalation for risk Dedicated Mental Vault storage No diagnosis No medication or treatment advice

Why a Mental Health Engine Inside Elisence

Sleep, blood sugar, hormones, environment and family stress are deeply connected. Without a mental-health layer, a digital health OS is incomplete. MHME lets Elisence understand:

  • how bad nights affect mood and self-care the next day,
  • how diabetes events and nutrition patterns relate to stress,
  • how hormonal phases influence energy and sensitivity,
  • how heat, pollution and digital overload shape emotional resilience.

Instead of launching yet another isolated meditation app, MHME lives inside Elisence: it sees the whole person and still stays within a safe, non-medical, regulator-ready envelope.

Design Contract & Legal Boundary

What MHME Will Never Do

  • No diagnosis of depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar, ADHD or any disorder.
  • No medication suggestions, dose changes or treatment plans.
  • No claims of cure, remission or clinical outcomes.
  • No pretending to be a therapist or crisis professional.

MHME is positioned as emotional wellness and self-help education, aligned with:

  • NHS DTAC and NICE guidance for digital support tools,
  • EU MDR non-MDSW “wellbeing” positioning in default mode,
  • FDA General Wellness & non-device CDS principles,
  • GCC / SFDA and national mental-health sensitivity rules.

Core Capabilities

Calm Now & Micro-Interventions

MHME will offer short, structured tools that users can trigger in moments of stress:

  • 30-second grounding and sensory focus
  • 1–3 minute breathing and reset exercises
  • 5–15 minute meditations for stress, sleep, focus and chronic illness fatigue
  • Simple CBT/DBT-inspired reflections and reframing, clearly marked as self-help

Daily Mood & Trend Awareness

Users can log emotion and energy in a simple way (icons / 1–5 scale, optional text). MHME groups this into non-clinical categories like:

  • current distress: low / moderate / high
  • trend: stable / improving / worsening

These signals power coaching and reminders, not diagnostic labels.

Risk & Escalation

What Happens When Risk Is High

When answers or language indicate significant distress, MHME:

  • stops normal coaching and meditation suggestions,
  • shows an escalation message in the user’s own language,
  • guides them toward helplines, clinicians or trusted people,
  • writes a high-level WORM audit event (without exposing private text).

The principle is clear: Elisence does not treat crisis through AI. It recognises distress and gently redirects to human care.

Mental Vault & Zero-Trust Model

All mental-health data sits in a dedicated Mental Vault:

  • separate encrypted tables and keys,
  • strict scopes and passports for any access,
  • self-only default visibility for citizens,
  • optional high-level trend view for clinicians, with explicit consent,
  • only anonymised aggregates for ministries and public-health teams.

This gives governments a concrete answer to “where is the mental data, and who can see it?” — without slowing down user experience.

Five-Language & Cultural Design

MHME is designed natively for EN / FA / AR / TR / RO. Scripts, voice text and UI copy are crafted for:

  • GCC and MENA cultural norms around mental health and faith,
  • European and UK communication styles,
  • family- and youth-safe language (teens, students, parents).

No live, machine-translated therapy claims; everything is curated and reviewed for tone, clarity and safety.

Real-Life Use Cases

  • Everyday stress & burnout: citizens trigger a Calm Now routine, track stress patterns and receive gentle, practical suggestions.
  • Sleep & mood: MHME links short sleep and late screens to next-day mood, and offers realistic wind-down ideas.
  • Women+ mood awareness: cycle-, pregnancy-, and postpartum-aware explanations and calm routines, without labels or blame.
  • Teens & students: quick focus resets, exam stress support, conflict cool-downs, under guardian/consent rules.
  • Chronic disease burden: empathetic support when diabetes or other conditions become emotionally heavy.
