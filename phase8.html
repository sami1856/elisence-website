PHASE 8

Advanced Health Engine (AHE)

GOVERNED MEDICAL INTELLIGENCE · NATIONAL HEALTH INFRASTRUCTURE

Phase 8 is the governed medical intelligence layer of Elisence – the point where Elisence stops being an app and becomes health infrastructure. It unifies signals from women’s health, blood, diabetes, behaviour, devices and environment into safe, non-diagnostic intelligence for citizens, clinicians, hospitals and ministries.

  • Multi-core health engine, OFF-by-default for high-risk features.
  • Role- and country-aware governance from day one.
  • Built to satisfy cautious regulators and demanding investors.

1. What Phase 8 Actually Is

Phase 8 (AHE – Advanced Health Engine) is not a single score and not a monolithic “AI brain”. It is a governed, multi-core health engine that:

  • Collects signals from women’s health, blood tests, diabetes, behaviour, devices and environment.
  • Normalises and interprets them in a safe, non-diagnostic, explainable way.
  • Produces calm, human-readable guidance for citizens and structured, auditable risk views for clinicians and ministries.
  • Runs high-risk medical features OFF by default, activatable only via explicit feature flags and governance.

Mission: reduce pressure on health systems by catching risk earlier, educating citizens better, and turning raw data into structured, trustworthy intelligence instead of noise.

2. Seven-Core Architecture

AHE is deliberately split into seven Cores. Each Core has its own risk profile, feature flags and governance. A problem in one Core never silently contaminates the others.

Core-1 — Women (11K)

  • Women’s contextual health signals (cycle, symptoms, pregnancy, menopause).
  • Powered by Women AHE 11K with separate citizen, clinician and ministry views.

Core-2 — Blood / Hematology

  • Unified blood profile (AHE_Blood_Profile_v1) used across Women+, Diabetes, GLP-1 and the Risk Engine.
  • Non-diagnostic hints around anaemia, bleeding, Rh-negative pregnancy and other safety-relevant patterns.

Core-3 — Diabetes & Metabolic

  • Signals from glucose/A1c, weight, GLP-1 response and adherence.
  • Patterns surfaced to clinicians and ministries; no automated prescribing.

Core-4 — Mental Health & Wellbeing

  • Mood, energy and sleep signals tied into the Dopamine Engine.
  • Safety rules for worrying combinations; always escalation, never diagnosis.

Core-5 — Kids & Family

  • Family context and child-safe signals, governed by guardians.
  • Connects motherhood journeys with Phase 10 (Kids+).

Core-6 — Environment / Context

  • Non-clinical context (heat, air quality, seasonality) that modulates risk interpretation.

Core-7 — Charts & Intelligence

  • Population-level dashboards, cohort views and AE radar for ministries and providers.
  • Fully governed; only anonymised, k-anonymous data leaves clinical scope.

Each Core has independent switches, audit trails and evidence packs. Countries can enable only the Cores they are comfortable with.

3. Governance & Security — Per Country, Per Role, Per Feature

Phase 8 carries the heaviest security and governance layer in the Elisence architecture.

3.1 Country-Level Control

  • Every request carries a Session Passport / IdP token with user_id, role_v8, org_id, country_code, access_tier, auth_strength.
  • country_code + access_tier define which Cores and dashboards are allowed.
  • Qatar, Saudi and other markets can run different activation mixes on the same engine.

3.2 Role-Based Security

  • Formal roles: citizen, clinician, clinic_admin, hospital_admin, ministry, research_partner.
  • Citizens see calm explanations; clinicians see structured risk; ministries see aggregates only.
  • Higher roles require stronger authentication (MFA, hardware keys, VPN).

3.3 Zero-Trust by Default

  • Gateway validates token signature, issuer, audience and expiry for every call.
  • Minimal claims forwarded as headers (e.g. X-Elisence-UserRole, X-Elisence-Country).
  • Any inconsistency → request rejected before touching clinical logic.

Result: regulators control who sees what, where and when — and they can turn features off in hours, not years.

4. Women+ & Blood as Strategic Backbones

Women’s health and blood are treated as first-class, not secondary signals.

4.1 Women AHE 11K

  • Citizen view: respectful, non-diagnostic explanations, always pushing towards professional review.
  • Clinician view: structured risk flags, patterns and checklist-style prompts.
  • Ministry view: population-level patterns (e.g. heavy bleeding, anaemia in pregnancy), anonymised.

4.2 Unified Blood Profile

  • AHE_Blood_Profile_v1 is the single schema used across Women+, Diabetes, GLP-1 and Risk Engine.
  • Non-diagnostic hints only, such as “this pattern may be consistent with low blood count – discuss with a clinician”.
  • Blood dashboards and cohort tools exist but are OFF by default, waiting for national governance.

5. Risk Engine — Explainable, Not Black-Box

The Phase 8 Risk Engine is our own explainable method, influenced by open frameworks (e.g. cardiovascular risk scores) but not a copy of any proprietary model.

5.1 Clinical Logic

  • Focus on cardiovascular, metabolic, kidney and chronic disease risk.
  • Combines demographics, long-term factors, symptoms, blood and behaviour into a governed risk picture.
  • Outputs for clinicians: structured risk levels with “why” and “which signals drove this”.

5.2 Citizen vs Clinician Outputs

  • Clinicians / ministries see structured risk and cohorts.
  • Citizens see no “10-year risk” numbers – only gentle prompts about lifestyle, check-ups and speaking to a doctor.

This keeps AHE clinically meaningful while remaining outside high-risk device classes in most markets.

6. Charts & Intelligence — Core-7 as National Radar

Core-7 turns AHE into a “national radar” for chronic disease and Women+ health.

  • Dashboards for diabetes, cardiovascular and kidney risk trends.
  • Women+ dashboards for heavy bleeding, pelvic pain, pregnancy signals (aggregated only).
  • Cohort comparisons by region, age, gender, BMI and medication type.
  • Impact evaluation of interventions (campaigns, clinics, new protocols).
  • Strict k-anonymity rules: small cohorts are hidden to prevent re-identification.

Built as compliance-first analytics, suitable for ministries, not as a flashy consumer BI toy.

7. Data Handling & Unified Encrypted Vault

AHE assumes a single encrypted health vault instead of scattered databases.

  • Encryption in transit and at rest; TLS everywhere.
  • Unified profiles linking blood, Women+, diabetes, behaviour, devices and risk outputs.
  • WORM audit logging for ingest, risk evaluations and dashboard access.
  • Separation of identifiers and clinical content for GDPR / PDPL compliance.
  • Export modes limited to anonymised, k-anonymous aggregates for research and public-health policy.

8. Stakeholder Views & Use Cases

8.1 Citizens

  • Use Elisence as a calm companion for Women+, GLP-1, sleep, mood and activity.
  • Get simple explanations and nudges to seek help sooner.
  • Keep full control over what is logged and what is shared.

8.2 Clinicians & Clinics

  • Receive structured pre-visit context and risk, not unstructured chatter.
  • See trends that are difficult to reconstruct inside a 10-minute appointment.

8.3 Hospitals

  • Use AHE dashboards to identify high-risk groups and prevent readmissions.
  • Target specialist resources where impact is highest.

8.4 Ministries & Governments

  • Deploy AHE as a national radar for chronic disease and Women+ health.
  • Choose which Cores and dashboards are enabled in which regions.
  • Run staged pilots before full national roll-out.

9. Regulatory Posture & Modes

AHE is designed for regulators who prefer caution and evidence.

9.1 Non-Medical Default Mode

  • Wellbeing and education only; no diagnoses, no dosing, no treatment plans.
  • Safe lifestyle guidance and general explanations.

9.2 Medical-Adjacent Mode (Restricted)

  • Structured risk intelligence and dashboards for clinicians and ministries.
  • High-risk modules (advanced Women 11K, stroke-like signals, blood heatmaps) behind feature flags.
  • Fail-closed behaviour: if governance isn’t set, features return controlled 503 with roadmap text.

This keeps Elisence deployable today while leaving a clear path to higher MDR / FDA classes when countries are ready.

10. Clear Limits & Honest Promises

AHE deliberately does not:

  • Replace doctors, clinics or hospitals.
  • Give autonomous diagnoses or prescriptions.
  • Offer emergency triage as a stand-alone service.
  • Guarantee clinical outcomes.

Instead, Phase 8 is a governed, explainable intelligence layer that sits beside health systems, reduces noise, and makes it easier for professionals and ministries to do their job.

11. One-Sentence Summary

Phase 8 – Advanced Health Engine (AHE) is Elisence’s multi-core, governed medical intelligence layer: a zero-trust, multi-language, regulator-friendly engine that turns fragmented signals from women’s health, blood, diabetes, behaviour and environment into safe guidance for citizens and structured intelligence for clinicians and ministries — without ever pretending to replace a doctor.
