Phase 14 — Life Map Companion

Life Map Companion for Blind & Low-Vision Users

Phase 14 is the future accessibility layer of Elisence: a voice-first, on-device spatial companion that turns the world into safe, respectful descriptions for blind and low-vision users. The roadmap and safety contract are fully designed; the implementation will follow in later phases.

Core Definition

What Phase 14 Is

Phase 14 is the Life Map Companion of Elisence. Instead of showing screens, it describes the world around the user:

  • Rooms and indoor spaces
  • Tables, food and menus while dining
  • People around the user, in a respectful way
  • Basic outdoor cues like curbs, crossings and obstacles

Its mission is simple and ambitious: “Turn the world into words — safely, beautifully, and in real time.”

Design Principles

Safety-first, never decision-making On-device by default Voice-first input and output Five-language foundation (EN / FA / AR / TR / RO) Accessibility-guideline aligned

Stakeholder View

Who Phase 14 Is For

  • Families and carers supporting daily independence
  • Orientation & Mobility instructors and schools for the blind
  • Ministries of Health, Education and Transport

Design Contract & Safety Locks

What Elisa Will Never Do

For blind and low-vision users, safety is non-negotiable. Phase 14 is built on strict promises:

  • No “It is safe” / “You can cross now” statements
  • No navigation decisions or route guarantees
  • No race, ethnicity, body or beauty descriptions
  • No face recognition or sensitive attribute inference

Elisa describes what she sees, where it appears and how it might relate to the user, but final decisions always stay with the person and their existing mobility tools.

How Processing Works

The planned architecture is on-device first for privacy and speed, with optional hybrid cloud only when users opt in for richer captions or advanced languages. All flows remain compatible with Apple and Android accessibility standards such as VoiceOver and TalkBack.

What Life Map Understands

Rooms & Indoor Spaces

Phase 14 is designed to build a mental map of indoor environments:

  • Room shape and basic layout
  • Doors, windows, hallways and major furniture
  • Obstacles such as chairs, tables or steps
  • Approximate distances and relative positions

The goal is not turn-by-turn navigation, but calm, useful descriptions that help users understand where they are and what surrounds them.

Dining & Food Intelligence

At home or in restaurants, the Life Map Companion is planned to:

  • Describe table layouts: plates, glasses, cutlery
  • Recognise common food items on plates
  • Read menus using on-device OCR
  • Offer simple, non-medical summaries like “lighter options”

Social & People Awareness

The system will focus on safe social context:

  • How many people are nearby and roughly where
  • High-level clothing colours and styles
  • Atmosphere cues like “it sounds busy” or “it feels quiet”

It will never describe race, body shape, attractiveness or other sensitive attributes.

Outdoor Safety Cues

Outdoors, Phase 14 is planned to spot:

  • Curbs, stairs and crosswalk markings
  • Parked and moving vehicles in the scene
  • Basic pedestrian signal states where feasible

All descriptions remain labelled as observations, not commands, and always reinforce the importance of canes, guides and training as the primary tools.

Personalisation & Modes

Three Levels of Detail

The Life Map Companion is designed to adapt to each person’s comfort level:

  • Minimal Mode: only essential cues such as doors, obstacles and number of people.
  • Standard Mode: adds furniture, table layouts and basic social information.
  • Rich Mode: fuller world-building, including clothing colours, table maps and atmosphere.

Users will be able to choose language, detail level, and how much social information they are comfortable hearing, and to save key environments such as home, work or a favourite café.

Roadmap & Implementation Status

Designed Now, Built Later

Phase 14 currently exists as a complete roadmap and design contract. Planned steps include:

  • Deep research with blind and low-vision communities and O&M experts
  • MVP for single-frame descriptions via a simple “describe this photo” flow
  • Spatial mapping engine for rooms, combined with spoken narratives
  • Dining and menu intelligence trials in real food environments
  • Ethical people-awareness module with strict filtering
  • Home mapping mode with local encrypted storage
  • Carefully limited outdoor cues (no navigation decisions)
  • Privacy, on-device defaults and WORM-style logging of key events
  • Pilot studies with schools, NGOs and ministries

None of these components are implemented yet; they are planned as a future execution wave once earlier phases have stabilised.

Ethics, Privacy & Compliance

Zero-Risk Accessibility Posture

From day one, Phase 14 is designed around a strict ethical boundary:

  • No unauthorised recording or sharing of images
  • No cloud processing without explicit consent
  • No storage of raw images beyond what is strictly needed
  • Encryption for all sensitive data and local maps
  • GDPR, UK DPA, GCC PDPL and HIPAA-safe (non-diagnostic) positioning

The goal is to empower users with more information about their world, without creating new privacy or safety risks.

Why Phase 14 Matters

National Impact & Strategic Value

For countries, ministries and investors, Phase 14 represents:

  • A flagship accessibility initiative that supports blind and low-vision communities at scale.
  • A technology asset that combines on-device AI, depth, segmentation and large-vision captioning under strict safety rules.
  • A natural partner for schools, public transport, hospitals and city planners.

Combined with Phase 13 (Voice Engine), it positions Elisence as a truly inclusive health and life companion, not just a clinical data platform.
