PHASE 14

Life Map Companion

BLIND & LOW-VISION · REAL-WORLD AWARENESS · VOICE-FIRST

Phase 14 is the Life Map Companion of Elisence — a real-time, voice-first accessibility engine for blind and low-vision users. Elisa converts the world into spoken understanding, turning rooms, people, tables and outdoor cues into safe, meaningful audio descriptions in five languages.

  • Room, social and dining awareness for blind & low-vision users.
  • On-device, privacy-first, non-navigational assistive AI.
  • Designed for national-scale accessibility programmes and smart cities.

1. What Phase 14 Is

Phase 14 is a real-time “Life Map Companion” for blind and low-vision users. It turns complex visual scenes into clear, concise spoken descriptions — always within a safe, non-medical, non-navigational boundary.

Elisa converts the world into spoken understanding through:

  • Room layout & furniture mapping — where doors, chairs, tables and key objects are.
  • People & social-context awareness — presence of people, group vs one-to-one, basic social cues.
  • Dining & table intelligence — what is on the table, basic item descriptions.
  • Menu reading (OCR + LLM) — reading and summarising menus in a gentle, blind-friendly format.
  • Indoor familiar-place guidance — descriptive orientation inside known homes/offices.
  • Outdoor hazard cues — cars, curbs, steps, basic obstacles (descriptive, not navigational).
  • Full voice-first interaction — EN / FA / AR / TR / RO.

Not navigation. Not medical. 100% assistive & safety-conscious.

4. Technical Advantage

Phase 14 is designed on top of a modern, privacy-first AI stack:

  • On-device AI (CoreML / TFLite) for low-latency and privacy.
  • Vision-Language Models (BLIP-2 / LLaVA class) for rich scene understanding.
  • Depth + segmentation + object detection fusion to understand rooms, tables and obstacles.
  • Blind-friendly narration engine:
    • Short, clear sentences.
    • Avoids overload and confusion.
    • Tuned for screen reader and headphone usage.
  • WORM-style ethical logging — only what is needed for safety, audits and debugging.
  • GDPR / PDPL / HIPAA-safe by design:
    • Minimal data retention.
    • No unnecessary face identification.
    • Strict consent and privacy modes.
