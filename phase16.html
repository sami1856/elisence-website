PHASE 16

Global Nutrition & Diet Intelligence Engine (GNIE)

GLOBAL NUTRITION · MULTI-DISEASE · CULTURE-AWARE

Phase 16 is the Global Nutrition & Diet Intelligence Engine (GNIE) of Elisence — the intelligence layer that finally connects food, disease, medication, environment and culture into one safe, explainable and government-grade system, while remaining non-diagnostic and non-prescriptive.

  • Nutrition Passport & Safety Profile for every user.
  • Culturally intelligent guidance for GCC, MENA, Europe and beyond.
  • Population-level dashboards for ministries and public health teams.

1. What Phase 16 Delivers

Phase 16 is where earlier Elisence phases converge on food. It turns everyday meals into structured, clinically-aware intelligence for citizens, clinicians and ministries — while remaining fully controllable via feature flags.

1.1 Individual Level (Citizens & Families)

  • A personal Nutrition Passport & Safety Profile for each user, built from:
    • medical conditions (diabetes, CKD, CVD, NAFLD, obesity),
    • women’s health context (cycle, pregnancy, postpartum, anaemia risk),
    • medications (GLP-1s, insulin, ACEi/ARB, diuretics, warfarin, etc.),
    • allergies, intolerances, religious and cultural dietary patterns,
    • behaviour data (weight, activity, sleep, mood) and environment (heat, AQI).
  • A daily nutrition coach that:
    • interprets what the user eats (text, barcode, later photos),
    • flags patterns such as excess salt, sugar, late-night high-GI meals, and dehydration risk,
    • suggests gentle, culturally realistic swaps — not restrictive crash diets or rigid rules.

1.2 Clinical Level (Doctors, Clinics, Hospitals)

  • A nutrition lens alongside vitals, labs and medications: structured tags such as ckd_high_potassium_pattern or htn_salt_excess_pattern.
  • Quick insight into how a patient actually eats between visits — without reading raw food logs.
  • Optional future Medical Mode (for clinicians only), controllable per country and deployment, ready to be uplifted to SaMD / CDS when regulatory cover exists.

1.3 Population Level (Ministries & Public Health Teams)

  • Fully anonymised nutrition intelligence dashboards for:
    • salt, sugar and ultra-processed food consumption by age, sex, region, disease group,
    • measuring the impact of policies (e.g. sugar taxes, salt reduction campaigns),
    • tracking Ramadan vs non-Ramadan patterns in GCC countries.

2. Why Phase 16 Comes After Diabetes, Women+ and Weight-Loss

Phase 16 is intentionally scheduled after the diabetes, women’s health and weight-loss phases because its value depends on them.

  • From Phase 9 (Diabetes) it inherits: glucose patterns, GLP-1 usage and metabolic risk profiles.
  • From Phase 7 (Women+) it understands: menstrual phases, pregnancy, postpartum, heavy bleeding and iron deficiency risk.
  • From Phase 3 & Phase 5 it uses: activity, sleep, and a proven Healthy Dopamine Engine to transform nutrition from “logging” into real behaviour change.
  • From Phase 8 (AHE) it reads: biomarkers such as lipids, creatinine, liver enzymes, ferritin and vitamin D — aligning nutrition guidance with real physiology.

GNIE is not “just another diet app”. It is a late-stage, high-leverage phase that amplifies the clinical relevance and real-world impact of everything Elisence has already built.

3. Cultural & Regional Intelligence (Eastern Foods & Ramadan)

Phase 16 is designed from day one to serve Eastern, GCC and multi-cultural populations — not only Western diets.

  • Deep support for Persian, Arabic/GCC, Turkish and Romanian cuisines in local languages, using real dish names: chelo kebab, kabsa, mandi, sarmale, lahmacun and more.
  • A respectful, evidence-based Ramadan Mode that:
    • understands suhoor, iftar and late-night snacks,
    • recognises heat and dehydration risk in GCC climates,
    • offers science-based, gentle advice in EN, FA, AR, TR, RO, always encouraging consultation with physicians for high-risk patients,
    • avoids issuing religious or clinical permissions.

This makes GNIE uniquely positioned for ministries in MENA, GCC and multi-cultural Europe, where Western-only diet apps consistently fail to adapt.

4. Regulatory, Legal & Zero-Trust Design

Phase 16 is regulatory-aligned by design, with a clear, safe path for ministries and health systems.

4.1 Default Mode — Wellness & Education

  • Wellness / education only, Medical Mode OFF by default.
  • Non-diagnostic, no prescribing, no claims of cure or disease reversal.
  • Nutrition insights framed as education and pattern-awareness, not as clinical decisions.

4.2 Feature-Flagged Medical Behaviour

  • GNIE_MEDICAL_MODE_ENABLED can be turned ON only for:
    • specific roles (doctors, hospitals),
    • specific deployments (e.g. NHS, SFDA-approved pilots),
    • under formal regulatory and liability frameworks with clear responsibilities.

4.3 Compliance Framing

  • NHS DTAC & NICE Evidence Standards Framework (UK).
  • EU MDR: wellness in default mode, MDSW-ready for future uplift.
  • FDA CDS / General Wellness guidance (US).
  • GCC PDPL, SFDA and regional digital-health rules (KSA, Qatar, UAE and others).

4.4 Zero-Trust Architecture

  • Passport-based identity on every request.
  • Strict role- and consent-based access to nutrition data and inferences.
  • WORM-style audit logs for all key nutrition events and recommendations.
  • Support for data residency and sovereign deployments (e.g. national clouds, dedicated regions).
