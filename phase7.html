PHASE 7

Women+ Health Intelligence

CYCLE · FERTILITY · PREGNANCY · MENOPAUSE · SAFE & RESPECTFUL

Phase 7 is the Women+ heart of Elisence. It listens to real-life cycles, symptoms and patterns across all life stages — from first periods to fertility, pregnancy, postpartum and menopause — and turns them into non-diagnostic, respectful, audit-ready insights that help women, families and clinicians understand what is happening, without ever replacing a doctor.

  • Designed with women’s safety, dignity and privacy as the first rule.
  • Non-diagnostic guidance anchored to NHS/NICE and global standards.
  • Five-language support: EN, FA, AR, TR, RO from day one.

1. What Phase 7 Really Is

Phase 7 is not “just a period tracker”. It is a structured, ethical Women+ Health Intelligence layer that understands patterns over time: cycles, symptoms, moods, energy, pain, bleeding, pregnancy context, postpartum recovery and menopause-related changes.

The goal is simple and serious: give women and girls a trustworthy companion that explains and organises what they are going through, while clearly reminding them that medical decisions must stay with qualified clinicians.

Phase 7 is built to work for:

  • Teenagers learning about their first periods in a safe, non-shaming way.
  • Women planning or avoiding pregnancy.
  • Pregnant users and new mothers tracking recovery and red-flag symptoms.
  • Women approaching peri- and post-menopause, understanding changes over time.
  • Clinics, ministries and NGOs that want ethical, anonymised insights — not surveillance.

2. What Is Already Built (Today’s Baseline)

Phase 7 is already more than a concept. The following foundations exist in code and tests:

2.1 Cycle & Symptom Engine

  • Structured events for cycle days, bleeding intensity, pain levels, mood tags and energy.
  • Support for irregular cycles, long / short cycles and anovulatory patterns (non-diagnostic).
  • Timeline views that help the user “see” their last months at a glance.

2.2 Safety & Tone Engine

  • Non-judgemental, non-shaming language across all outputs.
  • Always encourages rest, hydration, nutrition, movement and seeking help when needed.
  • Strict rules for pain, heavy bleeding and mental-health red flags: “talk to a professional”.

2.3 Guidance Cards Library (Multilingual)

  • Structured “Guidance Cards” for common scenarios (cycle changes, PMS, cramps, mood, sleep).
  • Five-language content: English, Farsi, Arabic, Turkish, Romanian (aligned but not identical).
  • Every card linked to a scenario ID and safety tier for audit and clinical review.

2.4 Audit & Governance Foundations

  • Every recommendation type is traceable to a rule ID and safety tier.
  • Designed to plug into Elisence’s WORM audit and k-anonymity privacy layers.
  • Clear separation between citizen-facing copy and clinician/ministry views.

All of this has been designed with NHS/NICE style safety language and global Women+ ethics in mind.

3. Women+ Journeys Covered

Phase 7 is built around real journeys, not just data points.

3.1 Everyday Cycle & PMS / PMDD

  • Logging of cycle start/end, flow and cramps without pressure or judgement.
  • Patterns of mood, sleep and cravings to help users plan self-care.
  • Gentle language around PMS / PMDD, always with mental-health safety gates.

3.2 Fertility Awareness (Non-Diagnostic)

  • Highlights fertile-window patterns in simple language, not medical predictions.
  • Clear disclaimers: not contraception, not a fertility treatment and not a substitute for a doctor.
  • Encourages couples to speak with clinicians for fertility concerns.

3.3 Pregnancy Context & Postpartum

  • Non-diagnostic flags for symptoms that should be discussed with midwife/doctor.
  • Supportive copy about fatigue, emotions, recovery and asking for help.
  • Postpartum focus on bleeding, mood, breastfeeding challenges and sleep deprivation.

3.4 Perimenopause & Menopause

  • Tracks changes in cycle length, hot flushes, sleep, mood and energy.
  • Explains in plain words what many women experience, without labels or stigma.
  • Encourages specialist review and HRT discussions where appropriate.

4. Safety, Ethics & Non-Diagnostic Boundaries

Women’s health has a long history of being ignored or over-controlled. Phase 7 tries to correct this by being honest, transparent and humble about what it can and cannot do.

4.1 Non-Diagnostic by Design

  • No labels such as “you have endometriosis” or “this is PCOS”.
  • Instead: “Your pattern looks like… here are questions to discuss with a clinician.”
  • Risk language is soft, supportive and clearly marked as information, not a verdict.

4.2 Emergency & Red-Flag Behaviour

  • Heavy bleeding, sudden severe pain, fainting, chest pain or suicidal thoughts are treated as critical.
  • Copy always pushes towards emergency services, hotlines or trusted clinicians.
  • No self-harm content, no dieting pressure, no weight-shaming.

4.3 Privacy & Control

  • No secret sharing of personal timelines with employers, insurers or family.
  • When data is used for research or ministry dashboards, it is anonymised and k-anonymous.
  • Users can choose what to log, what to keep private and what to share with their clinician.

4.4 Cultural & Language Sensitivity

  • Copy is adapted for EN/FA/AR/TR/RO, respecting local norms without hiding medical truth.
  • No moral judgement on sex, fertility choices or relationship status.
  • Focus is always on health, safety, dignity and autonomy of the user.

5. How Phase 7 Connects to the Rest of Elisence

Phase 7 is not an isolated app; it is deeply integrated into the Elisence ecosystem.

5.1 With Phase 3 (Weight & GLP-1)

  • Understands how medications like GLP-1 can affect cycles or appetite (non-diagnostic).
  • Helps users see their weight, exercise and cycle patterns together in one place.

5.2 With Phase 6 (Global Medication Brain)

  • Uses the medication engine to understand contraceptives, hormonal treatments and other drugs.
  • Never changes or recommends medication; only helps users remember and discuss patterns.

5.3 With Phase 8 / AHE (Advanced Health Engine)

  • Feeds anonymised pattern data into risk models for public-health level insights.
  • Supports ministry dashboards (e.g., anaemia, heavy-bleeding patterns) without exposing identities.

5.4 With Phase 10 (Families & Kids+)

  • Bridges mothers’ health with family wellbeing in a safe, opt-in way.
  • Supports educational content for parents and daughters about puberty and cycles.

6. Why Phase 7 Matters for Investors & Ministries

Women’s health is a fast-growing, under-served global market — but it must be handled with respect, science and regulation. Phase 7 is designed for that balance.

6.1 Massive, Real & Measurable Need

  • Billions of women with cycles, pregnancies and menopause transitions.
  • High rates of pain, anaemia, undiagnosed conditions and late diagnoses.
  • Low access to trusted, culturally aligned, multi-language tools.

6.2 Differentiation vs Standard Period Apps

  • Most apps are ad-driven trackers with weak science and poor privacy.
  • Elisence Phase 7 is governance-first, audit-ready and ministry-grade.
  • Focus on long-term partnerships with health systems, not quick user acquisition.

6.3 Regulatory-Friendly Design

  • Non-diagnostic, wellness-aligned posture for EU MDR and FDA CDS frameworks.
  • Structured evidence packs, hazard logs and WORM audit for NHS and GCC.
  • Clear path for a future clinician-only decision-support module if required.

6.4 Social Impact & Brand

  • Positions Elisence as a platform that truly cares about women’s health and dignity.
  • Attractive for NGOs, global foundations and CSR projects.
  • Builds trust that carries into other phases (AHE, Kids+, Elderly, etc.).

7. Limitations (Honest & Transparent)

Today, Phase 7 deliberately does not:

  • Diagnose conditions such as endometriosis, PCOS, PMDD or infertility.
  • Give direct treatment plans or prescriptions.
  • Guarantee pregnancy or contraception success.
  • Replace gynaecologists, midwives, mental-health professionals or GPs.
  • Provide emergency medical triage or crisis counselling by itself.

These limits are intentional. They protect users, protect clinicians and keep Elisence inside a safe, globally deployable regulatory perimeter.
