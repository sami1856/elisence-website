Non-Diagnostic Health Intelligence Policy

Elisence is a non-diagnostic platform. It never replaces a doctor, it never makes medical diagnoses, and it never gives prescriptions or drug dosing advice. Instead, it offers education, probabilities and safety guidance in clear, human language.

This page explains how the non-diagnostic boundary works across Women+, Diabetes, Nutrition, Family and all other phases – for citizens, ministries, regulators and clinical partners.

Non-Diagnostic Education-Only Guidance Safety First No Prescriptions No Diagnoses Explainable Rules

1. What “Non-Diagnostic” Means in Elisence

When Elisence talks to a user – in text, in voice, or inside a demo – it always stays within a strict education-only boundary.

  • No diagnosis: the system never says “you have X” or “this means diabetes / cancer / depression.”
  • No prescriptions: the system never recommends specific drugs or changes to dose, timing or combinations.
  • No medical orders: the system never tells users to stop or start medicines on its own.
  • Education and safety: the system explains patterns, probabilities and when to seek human help.

The goal is to support clinicians and ministries, not to replace them.

2. Language Boundaries – Allowed vs Blocked Phrases

The Elisence engines use safety rules to control how messages are written. Some styles of message are allowed; others are blocked by design.

  • Allowed “Your readings look higher than usual. Please speak to your doctor or diabetes nurse for a proper clinical assessment.”
  • Allowed “These symptoms can sometimes be a sign of something important. If you feel worse, contact your local emergency services.”
  • Allowed “This is educational information, not a diagnosis. It does not replace your doctor.”
  • Blocked “You have diabetes type 2. Start taking 850 mg metformin twice a day.”
  • Blocked “Stop taking your current medicine. You do not need it anymore.”
  • Blocked “You definitely do not need to see a doctor for this.”

These boundaries are enforced inside the engines and verified by automated tests.

3. How This Works in Women+, Diabetes and Nutrition

The non-diagnostic policy applies across all phases, with special attention in:

  • Women+ (Phase 7): cycle, pain, pregnancy and mental wellbeing modules give clear education and safety messages but never label a user with a clinical diagnosis.
  • Diabetes & Metabolic (Phase 9): the engine can describe patterns (for example, “your readings are often above your target range”) and advise users to see their clinician, but it never changes treatment plans or gives dosing advice.
  • Nutrition (Phase 16): GNIE suggests balanced, culturally appropriate meal ideas and risk-aware tips, but it does not prescribe therapeutic diets in place of a nutritionist.

In every case, Elisence points back to real clinicians for decisions.

4. How Users See the Non-Diagnostic Boundary

For citizens, the boundary is visible and explicit, not hidden in legal text. The interface and voice companion Elisa regularly remind users that:

  • Elisence is for education, tracking and support.
  • It does not replace doctors, nurses or emergency services.
  • Urgent or worrying symptoms must be discussed with a human clinician.
  • Emergency situations require local emergency numbers, not the app.

This is reflected in the wording of messages, the design of buttons, and the way alerts are framed in Women+, Diabetes, Family and other modules.

5. Regulatory Alignment (NHS, EU, GCC, Qatar)

The non-diagnostic posture is part of Elisence’s alignment with multiple frameworks:

  • NHS & EU: clear separation from diagnostic devices, avoiding medical claims.
  • FDA CDS principles: keeping the human clinician in control of decisions.
  • GCC & Qatar PDPL: treating health intelligence as sensitive, regulated data.
  • Global deployment: being safe to use across jurisdictions without conflicting claims.

Ministries and regulators can combine this policy with the technical evidence in the Evidence Hub to see how these rules are implemented in code and tests.

6. Engine-Level Enforcement & Traceability

Non-diagnostic behaviour is not just a promise; it is part of the engine design. Each response can carry internal metadata (for example: rule_id, trace_id, safety_level) so that outputs can be traced back to the exact rule that generated them.

  • Rule-based responses: engines use curated rule sets for messages.
  • Safety levels: responses are tagged (for example: general, attention, emergency-advice-to-call-services).
  • WORM audit: critical events can be written into append-only logs for later review.
  • Test coverage: automated tests verify that high-risk patterns never produce diagnostic or prescriptive text.

This makes the non-diagnostic boundary auditable – an important part of working with ministries and hospitals.

7. Demo & Synthetic Journeys

In the protected demo environment, the non-diagnostic rules are still fully active. In addition, all journeys are synthetic – based on scripted examples, not real patients.

  • Visitors see realistic scenarios, but no real personal data.
  • Every scenario is clearly marked as a demonstration only.
  • The same language rules apply: no diagnoses, no prescriptions, no dosing advice.

Together with Zero-Trust and Cloudflare Access, this creates a safe space where ministries, investors and partners can explore the platform without clinical risk.

8. Summary

Elisence is built to support people, families, clinicians and ministries – not to replace them. By staying strictly non-diagnostic, the platform can safely scale across countries, languages and health systems while respecting clinical boundaries and legal frameworks.
