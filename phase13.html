Phase 13 — Voice & Zero-Typing Engine

Talking to Elisence, Safely, in Five Languages

Phase 13 is the voice and zero-typing layer of Elisence. It lets people talk to Elisa in natural language, while keeping every step non-diagnostic, auditable and safe for real-world health journeys across English, Farsi, Arabic, Turkish and Romanian.

Core Definition

What Phase 13 Is

Phase 13 is Elisence’s Voice & Zero-Typing Engine. It turns speech into safe actions, so users can say:

  • “Elisa, show me my diabetes dashboard.”
  • “My bleeding is very heavy and I feel dizzy.”
  • “Read this medicine label for me.”
  • “Take me to Women’s pain information.”

The engine converts voice to text, text to intent, and intent to governed, non-diagnostic responses.

Design Principles

Zero-Trust by default Non-diagnostic, non-prescriptive NHS / EU MDR / FDA CDS boundary-aware 5-language, accent-aware foundation BBVP: Build, Back, Verify, Prove

Stakeholder View

Who Benefits From Phase 13?

  • Citizens who prefer speaking instead of typing
  • Women+ users describing symptoms in their own words
  • People living with diabetes or chronic conditions
  • Blind and low-vision users needing voice-first access
  • Clinicians and ministries that want safe, auditable voice flows

Safety Guarantees

No diagnosis, ever No dosage or treatment decisions Uncertainty = clarify or safe fallback WORM audit for safety events No “It is safe / unsafe to cross” No navigation decisions

How the Voice Engine Works

From Speech to Safe Action

Phase 13 follows a clear, inspectable pipeline:

  • Audio capture on the phone or headset
  • Speech-to-text (on-device where possible, edge or cloud as backup)
  • Intent extraction using hybrid rules and models
  • Safety guard checking symptoms, roles and country rules
  • Routing to Women+, Diabetes, AHE, Blind-Assist or coaching engines
  • Localized response in the user’s language, text and voice
  • WORM logging for safety-relevant decisions

Each step is testable and traceable. Phase 13 is not a black-box assistant; it is a governed control layer on top of Elisence’s health engines.

Noise, Reliability & Uncertainty

Built for Real Homes and Hospitals

Voice systems often break in noisy, stressful environments. Phase 13 treats this as a first-class problem, not an afterthought:

  • Uses more than one STT source when allowed, and compares meanings
  • Flags inputs as uncertain when transcripts do not agree
  • Never silently guesses around safety-related words

When uncertainty is detected around symptoms like heavy bleeding, chest pain or severe dizziness, Elisa will ask for clarification rather than acting as if she is sure. If doubt remains, the system falls back to generic, guideline-safe advice such as seeking urgent care, instead of making a risky decision.

Accents, Dialects & Five Languages

Accent-Aware, Not Accent-Blind

Phase 13 is designed for English, Farsi, Arabic, Turkish and Romanian, with space for multiple accents and dialects within each language. Every session carries:

  • a language code (en, fa, ar, tr, ro)
  • a region hint (UK, EU, GCC and others)
  • an accent hint where available (for example Gulf Arabic vs other variants)

Vocabulary is hybrid: shared clinical and wellness terms plus regional phrases, with conservative behaviour when confidence is low. The engine prefers to admit uncertainty rather than pretend it understands every accent perfectly from day one.

Non-Medical, Non-Diagnostic Boundary

Voice as a Companion, Not a Doctor

Even when users describe serious symptoms, Phase 13 is carefully positioned as a wellness and guidance companion, not a diagnostic tool:

  • No statements like “You have anaemia” or “You are having a stroke”
  • No direct dosage or medication decisions
  • No replacement of clinicians or emergency services

Instead, the engine recognises categories of concern and delivers general, legally safe guidance, encouraging users to seek proper care when needed. This keeps Elisence on the safe side of NHS, FDA CDS, EU MDR and GCC rules.

Blind-Assist & Accessibility

Describing the World, Not Controlling It

Together with the accessibility phases, Phase 13 acts as the voice of Elisence for blind and low-vision users:

  • Reading medicine labels, menus and signs aloud
  • Describing scenes in front of the camera in soft language (“It looks like…”, “There may be…”)
  • Helping users move through Elisence without typing or complex gestures

It deliberately avoids giving precise navigation commands or promises of safety. Existing mobility tools remain in charge; Elisence’s role is to describe, not to direct.

Latency, Performance & Cost

Fast Enough to Feel Human

To feel natural, voice needs to respond quickly. Phase 13 is engineered with strict latency budgets:

  • On-device speech recognition where possible
  • Lightweight intent and safety checks
  • Streaming voice responses so users hear the first words in under a second in typical conditions

On-device and edge processing keep both latency and cost under control, so voice can scale to national programmes without becoming unaffordable.

Security, Zero-Trust & Audit

Every Utterance Under Control

Phase 13 inherits the security spine of Elisence:

  • Every request is tied to a Phase 11 passport and role
  • Actions are constrained by scopes and jurisdiction rules
  • Safety-critical events are logged in append-only WORM audit trails

This means regulators and ministries can see, in detail, how voice inputs were interpreted, which rules fired and why certain responses were given.

Integration With Other Phases

Voice Front Door for All Journeys

Phase 13 does not work in isolation. It is the voice front door for:

  • Women+ health journeys (Phase 7)
  • Diabetes and metabolic health (Phase 9)
  • Social profiles and messages (Phase 10)
  • Security and passports (Phase 11)
  • Real-time calls (Phase 12)
  • Accessibility and mental health phases beyond

In practice, it lets people say what they need, in their own words, and then hands off to the right engine under clear, auditable rules.

Government & Investor View

Accessible, Yet Legally Defensible

For governments and health systems, Phase 13 offers voice access without creating unmanaged risk:

  • Clear non-diagnostic boundary
  • Evidence-based validation and stress testing
  • Transparent metrics on accuracy, latency and safety fallbacks

For investors, it turns Elisence from a “text app” into a genuinely inclusive companion that people can speak to, in their own language and accent, while still satisfying strict regulatory expectations.

In one sentence: Phase 13 makes Elisence a true voice-first health companion — people talk, Elisa listens and responds safely, in five languages, under a Zero-Trust, non-diagnostic architecture that regulators and investors can understand and trust.
