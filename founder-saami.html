Saami Salami Asl — Founder of Elisence

Saami Salami Asl is the founder of Elisence, a London-based global health and AI platform focused on prevention, trusted health intelligence, continuity of care, women’s health, metabolic health, family wellbeing, and human-centred digital health systems.

The Beginning

Before building Elisence, I studied Electrical Engineering. That foundation shaped the way I think: structured, logical, and based on systems that must work under pressure.

Later, I became a lawyer — and learned how to navigate complexity, evidence, truth, institutions, and the importance of protecting people.

These two worlds — engineering and law — became the blueprint for Elisence: a platform that must be technically strong, human-centred, and legally clear.

The Human Story

Elisence was not born in a boardroom. It was born in real life — through love, responsibility, resilience, and the belief that health technology should support people with clarity and care.

As a father, I wanted to build a world where people, families, and children have tools that guide them safely through life. Tools that support discipline, understanding, emotional strength, prevention, and health.

Elisence became that world.

The Vision

Elisence is a 17-phase global platform designed to connect:

  • Families
  • Clinicians
  • Hospitals and ministries
  • Researchers and partners

It is built on Zero-Trust security, WORM logging, trusted health intelligence, and non-diagnostic AI that respects human boundaries.

Why I Continue Building

Because millions of people deserve better health guidance. Because families deserve tools that do not manipulate them. Because governments deserve trusted, honest, auditable health intelligence. Because children deserve a world designed for their wellbeing, not for addiction.

And because my daughter deserves to grow up in a world where technology protects humanity — not replaces it.

In One Sentence

Elisence is my life’s work — a platform built from heart, science, engineering, law, responsibility, and a father’s promise.
