PHASE 2

Medication Choice & Safety Engine

Phase 2 is the medication choice brain of Elisence. It does not prescribe or diagnose. Instead, it organises and compares medication options — starting with GLP-1 therapies — in a safe, transparent way for users, clinicians, and, in future, ministries of health.

What Phase 2 Is

From “Which drug is right for me?” to a clear, structured, safety-first view.

Phase 2 is designed to answer one of the hardest questions in modern metabolic and chronic care: “which medication, at which time, for which person, under which conditions?”

It focuses first on GLP-1 and related metabolic therapies and then extends to broader drug classes. The engine can:

  • Compare different GLP-1 options (e.g. Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) from a safety and suitability angle
  • Highlight key differences such as dosing rhythm, indication, common side effects, and escalation patterns
  • Map high-level benefit–risk considerations (non-diagnostic, non-prescriptive)
  • Prepare structured summaries that clinicians can use during real consultations

How It Feels for Users

For individuals and families, Phase 2 is not a medical calculator. It is a clarity layer sitting on top of confusing drug names and claims.

  • Plain-language explanations of what each medication is intended to do
  • Simple comparisons (e.g. “weekly injection vs daily tablet”, “weight focus vs diabetes focus”)
  • Safety prompts like “this is a conversation to have with your doctor if you have X or Y condition”
  • Guidance delivered through Elisa in a calm, non-judgmental way

Phase 2 never tells users which medication to take, never adjusts doses, and never replaces the doctor–patient decision.

How It Works for Clinicians & Clinics

For clinicians, Phase 2 is a structured background engine that helps them:

  • See a consolidated view of relevant medication options for a given profile (e.g. GLP-1, SGLT2, or others later)
  • Review key compatibility and high-level caution markers (e.g. kidney disease, pregnancy, severe GI issues)
  • Receive a concise, printable or shareable summary to support shared decision-making with the patient

Over time, Phase 2 can connect to national formularies, hospital guidelines, and health-authority restrictions, while keeping the final choice firmly with clinicians.

Safety, Limits & Governance

Phase 2 follows strict safety boundaries:

  • Non-diagnostic: never labels or confirms a disease.
  • Non-prescriptive: never chooses or adjusts a specific medication or dose.
  • Evidence-grounded: anchored in guideline-style data and transparent sources.
  • Zero-trust & audit-ready: every query and generated summary can be logged and later inspected through Phase 4 and Phase 8.

The engine is built so that ministries, hospitals and regulators can see clearly how medication comparisons are generated, and where its boundaries are.

Connection to the Wider Elisence Platform

Phase 2 does not work in isolation; it is designed to sit between real-world data and real-world decisions.

  • Phase 1: uses the weight, symptoms and GLP-1 history to add context to choices
  • Phase 3: converts chosen treatment plans into daily care, reminders and follow-up
  • Phase 7: respects women’s health specifics when considering safety and pathways
  • Phase 8: feeds anonymised patterns into risk engines and national dashboards
  • Phase 9: links diabetes-specific needs (HbA1c, hypoglycaemia risk, comorbidities)
  • Phase 16: aligns medication decisions with nutrition and lifestyle recommendations

In short, Phase 2 is the medication choice layer inside Elisence – always supportive, always transparent, always within safe boundaries.
