Interview — Women+ Health and Phase 7

A conversation with Saami Salami Asl about respectful women’s wellbeing, trusted health intelligence, privacy, and human-centred digital health

Interviewee: Saami Salami Asl · Founder & Architect of Elisence
Topic: Women+ Phase 7 — women’s wellbeing, cycles, pregnancy, postpartum support, trusted health intelligence, and respectful digital experiences.

1. Why Create a Dedicated Women+ Phase?

Q: Why create a dedicated Women+ phase instead of treating women’s health as a feature?

A: Women’s wellbeing is complex, deeply personal, and connected across many stages of life. Cycles, pregnancy, postpartum experiences, wellbeing, emotional health, pain experiences, hormones, and everyday life are often interconnected.

Phase 7 is designed around the belief that women’s wellbeing deserves thoughtful design, respectful language, privacy, trusted health intelligence, and a more human-centred experience.

2. Respectful Support and Clear Boundaries

Q: What role is Women+ Phase 7 designed to play?

A: The intention is to support awareness, understanding, and more informed wellbeing experiences. Elisence is designed to help women notice patterns, organise information, and better understand changes over time.

The focus remains on trusted health intelligence, wellbeing awareness, continuity, and respectful support.

Q: Why is respectful language so important in women’s wellbeing?

A: Language matters deeply. Many people have experienced frustration, misunderstanding, fear, or dismissal in health settings.

Elisence is designed around calm, respectful, and supportive communication. The intention is to help women feel heard, respected, and supported rather than overwhelmed or judged.

3. Wellbeing Across Different Life Stages

Q: How does Phase 7 approach pregnancy and postpartum wellbeing?

A: Pregnancy and postpartum experiences are approached with care, sensitivity, and respect. The intention is to support awareness, wellbeing, continuity, and clearer understanding of experiences over time.

Elisence is designed to help women feel more supported while encouraging healthy conversations, awareness, and wellbeing-focused continuity.

Q: Does emotional wellbeing matter in Women+ Phase 7?

A: Emotional wellbeing matters enormously. Women’s experiences are shaped not only by physical health, but also by emotional wellbeing, stress, relationships, sleep, life changes, and everyday pressures.

The goal is to support more human-centred wellbeing experiences that feel calmer, clearer, and easier to navigate.

4. Privacy and Trust

Q: Women’s wellbeing information is highly sensitive. How is trust approached?

A: Trust, privacy, dignity, and respectful handling of information are central to the philosophy behind Elisence.

Women+ experiences are designed around privacy-aware, governance-conscious, and human-centred digital health principles intended to help people feel safer and more supported.

Q: What role does personal control play?

A: Personal wellbeing journeys are deeply individual. The intention is for people to feel more informed, more supported, and more in control of how they engage with trusted health intelligence and wellbeing experiences.

5. What Does Success Look Like for Women+ Phase 7?

Q: When you imagine success for Women+ Phase 7, what do you hope to see?

A: Success means helping women feel more supported, more understood, and less alone in navigating wellbeing experiences.

It means calmer conversations, stronger continuity, more trusted health experiences, and digital support that feels respectful, human, and genuinely useful.
