Interview — Phase 10: Social Layer, Messaging & Security

A conversation with Saami Salami Asl about trusted communication, privacy, and human-centred health experiences

Interviewee: Saami Salami Asl · Founder & Architect of Elisence
Topic: Phase 10 — trusted communication, messaging, public profiles, attachments, privacy, and governance.

1. Why Does a Health Platform Need a Communication Layer?

Q: Why include communication and social features inside a health platform?

A: Health is not only appointments, records, or information. It is also support, encouragement, family conversations, shared wellbeing, and trusted communication. If health-related communication happens only outside structured environments, people may lose visibility, trust, continuity, and clarity around sensitive information. Phase 10 is designed to support communication inside a more privacy-aware and governance-conscious environment.

2. How Is Phase 10 Different From Traditional Messaging?

Q: What makes Phase 10 different from traditional messaging platforms?

A: Traditional messaging platforms are often optimised for engagement and speed. Phase 10 is designed around trust, privacy, transparency, and responsible communication.

It is designed around:
• privacy-aware communication preferences
• trusted and structured identity contexts
• responsible handling of shared information
• communication experiences designed for wellbeing rather than unhealthy engagement

Q: Can people publicly share health-related content?

A: Public participation is approached carefully and responsibly. Elisence is designed to encourage supportive, educational, and wellbeing-focused experiences while respecting privacy, trust, safety, and clear boundaries.

3. Public Profiles and Trusted Identity

Q: What is the purpose of public profiles?

A: Public profiles are designed to help people, professionals, and organisations participate in more transparent and trusted ways. The focus is on clear identity, trust, boundaries, and responsible participation within a health-oriented environment.

Q: What about privacy for families and everyday users?

A: Elisence is designed with privacy-aware defaults and user choice. People decide what they share, when they share it, and with whom. The intention is to support connection and encouragement while respecting dignity, wellbeing, and personal boundaries.

4. Attachments and Trusted Information Sharing

Q: How does Elisence approach shared documents and files?

A: Sensitive information deserves careful handling. Elisence is designed around trusted information-sharing principles where documents, images, and shared content can exist within privacy-aware and governance-conscious experiences.

The intention is to support:
• responsible access
• privacy-conscious sharing
• trusted communication contexts
• safer long-term continuity of information

5. Avoiding Harmful Engagement Patterns

Q: Many platforms become addictive or stressful. How is Elisence different?

A: Elisence is designed around calm, respectful, and supportive engagement. The focus is not on pressure, comparison, or endless attention loops.

Instead, the goal is to encourage:
• supportive interactions
• wellbeing and continuity
• healthier communication experiences
• calmer and more respectful digital environments

6. How Does Phase 10 Connect to Elisence?

Q: How does Phase 10 fit into the wider Elisence ecosystem?

A: Phase 10 supports connection, communication, continuity, and trusted participation across the wider Elisence platform.

It is intended to help support wellbeing, family participation, prevention, trusted communication, and more human-centred digital health experiences.

7. What Should Organisations Understand About Phase 10?

Q: What is the key message for organisations evaluating Elisence?

A: The key message is that Phase 10 is not simply messaging. It is designed as a trusted communication and identity layer intended for privacy-aware, governance-conscious, and human-centred health experiences.
