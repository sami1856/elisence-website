Timeline — Building Elisence

Key milestones in the journey of Saami Salami and the Elisence platform.

Early Concept — Connecting Health & Family Life

Concept Stage

The first idea for Elisence emerged from real experiences with fragmented health systems, complex legal processes, and the emotional reality of families trying to manage care.

Phase 1–3 — Core Infrastructure & Care Engine

Foundations

Saami focused on building a solid backbone: core APIs, safety boundaries, and a care/weight-loss engine that could later support more advanced phases.

Phase 4 — Elisence Core Infrastructure

Stability & Governance

Work on Phase 4 centred on governance, interoperability, and performance — preparing Elisence for real-world expectations from clinics and ministries.

Phase 7 — Women+ Health & Life Companion

Women+ Focus

Saami designed the Women+ engine as a respectful, non-diagnostic companion for cycle health, pregnancy, postpartum journeys, and pain/symptom awareness.

Phase 8 — AHE & Zero-Trust / Risk Engines

Advanced Health Engine

The AHE work brought together risk modelling, safety contracts, and Zero-Trust architecture, making sure Elisence behaves in a predictable, testable, and auditable way.

Phase 9 — Diabetes & GLP-1 Intelligence

Chronic Care

Diabetes intelligence was built as a way to structure events, patterns, and lifestyle links — supporting clinicians and patients without giving direct treatment decisions.

Phases 15–17 — Families, Nutrition, Mental Health

0–18, GNIE, CalmNow

Saami extended Elisence into the Family Ecosystem (0–18), Global Nutrition Intelligence, and Mental Health & Calm support — always with clear non-diagnostic boundaries.

Public Web & Investor-Ready Layer

Public Presence

With the technical core maturing, Saami began building the public website, reports, videos, and founder materials to present Elisence to ministries, investors, and partners.
