Interview — Saami Salami Asl, Founder of Elisence

A conversation about resilience, purpose, and building a more human approach to digital health

Interviewee: Saami Salami Asl · Founder & Architect of Elisence
Topic: Prevention, trusted health intelligence, continuity of care, and the human-centred philosophy behind Elisence.

1. Who Is Saami Salami Asl?

Q: For people meeting you for the first time, how do you introduce yourself?

A: I am Saami Salami Asl, Founder and Architect of Elisence. I believe technology should help people feel safer, more informed, and more supported — especially during moments when health, wellbeing, and family life feel uncertain or complex.

2. How Did Human Experience Shape Elisence?

Q: What experiences influenced the creation of Elisence?

A: Elisence was shaped by observing how fragmented, stressful, and difficult systems can sometimes feel for people and families. These experiences helped reinforce a belief that technology should become clearer, calmer, more transparent, and more human.

Q: Did this influence how Elisence approaches families and wellbeing?

A: Yes. Elisence is built around the idea that health support should reduce stress, strengthen understanding, and help people feel more supported within safe and trusted boundaries.

3. Why Build a Health Ecosystem Instead of a Single App?

Q: Why build a larger health platform instead of one standalone application?

A: Real life is interconnected. Prevention, wellbeing, nutrition, family support, metabolic health, women’s health, communication, and continuity of care all influence one another. Elisence was designed as a connected global health platform rather than a disconnected tool.

4. What Does Elisence Represent?

Q: What is the core idea behind Elisence?

A: Elisence is designed to help healthcare feel more connected, preventative, supportive, transparent, and human. It aims to help people, families, clinicians, and healthcare systems navigate health with greater confidence, understanding, and continuity.

5. Balancing Technology, Responsibility, and Humanity

Q: How do you balance systems thinking, responsibility, and human needs?

A: I believe health technology must combine structure, safety, responsibility, and empathy. Technology alone is not enough. Systems should be understandable, transparent, and designed around human wellbeing rather than complexity.

6. What Do You Hope People Understand About Elisence?

Q: What do you hope people understand when they discover Elisence?

A: I hope people understand that Elisence is being built with long-term purpose — to support prevention, trusted health intelligence, continuity of care, family wellbeing, and more human-centred health experiences.

7. What Keeps You Motivated?

Q: Building a global health platform is difficult. What keeps you motivated?

A: The belief that health technology can become kinder, clearer, safer, and more useful for people. If Elisence can help people feel more supported, informed, and connected to health, then the effort is meaningful.

8. What Message Would You Leave for Future Users?

Q: If future users read this years from now, what would you want them to know?

A: You deserve systems that respect dignity, privacy, understanding, and humanity. Elisence is being built to help health feel more human — calmer, more supportive, and more connected to real life.
