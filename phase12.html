Phase 12 — Real-Time Voice & Video Connection

Safe Voice & Video Calls for Real Lives

Phase 12 is the real-time communication layer of Elisence. It turns profiles, messages and care journeys into live, human conversations — through secure voice and video calls that are designed for health, not for social noise.

Core Definition

What Phase 12 Is

Phase 12 is Elisence’s real-time voice and video engine. It provides:

  • One-to-one voice and video calls between citizens and trusted professionals
  • Family and carer calls around a shared health journey
  • Clinician-to-clinician calls for coordination, where allowed
  • Group sessions and webinars under ministry control

Technically, it is built on a WebRTC-style connectivity layer, secured by Phase 11 passports and integrated with Phase 10 profiles and messaging.

Design Principles

Zero-Trust, Passport-Only Access Non-diagnostic, Non-prescriptive End-to-end encryption aware Low-bandwidth and mobile-first Culturally respectful for GCC

Stakeholder View

Who Uses Phase 12?

  • Citizens needing voice support instead of text
  • Parents, teens and carers coordinating around care
  • Doctors, nurses, psychologists and nutritionists
  • Clinics and hospitals running remote sessions
  • Ministries running official health webinars

Safety & Governance

Passport + role + scope required Recording OFF by default Clear consent for any recording No anonymous calls No “open hotline” style random calls No public video rooms

Call Types & Use Cases

Citizen & Family Calls

For citizens and families, Phase 12 offers a simple way to speak with trusted people when text is not enough:

  • Parent and teen discussing a Women+ or mental health journey with a coach
  • Family member joining a remote check-in around diabetes or medication adherence
  • Carer speaking with a clinic about practical, non-diagnostic support

All calls are tied to real profiles and clear expectations: what this call can and cannot be used for.

Professional Calls

For clinicians and wellness professionals, Phase 12 provides a channel that is:

  • identity-verified via Phase 11 passports
  • connected to Elisence profiles and attachments
  • guarded by scopes that define which roles may call whom

Ministries can restrict which call types are enabled in each country or programme.

How Calls Are Started

From Profiles and Messages

Phase 12 is not a separate app. Instead, calls appear naturally where they make sense:

  • From a professional profile in Phase 10 (if calling is enabled)
  • From a secure message thread when both sides agree to escalate to a call
  • From a family view where carers are already connected to a user’s journey

Every call must pass through Phase 11: passport validation, role checks, scope checks and replay protection, before any media connection is attempted.

Technical & Network Architecture

Real-Time Engine

Under the surface, Phase 12 uses a WebRTC-style engine with:

  • STUN/TURN-like relays for difficult networks
  • Adaptive bitrate and resolution for low-bandwidth users
  • Support for voice-only mode when video is not possible or not desired

The goal is simple: “it just works” on typical home and mobile connections, including conditions common in GCC and wider regions.

Encryption & Identity

Media streams are encrypted in transit, and sessions are anchored to:

  • a valid X-Passport (Phase 11)
  • clear roles (citizen, teen, doctor, clinic, ministry)
  • scopes that define what kind of call is permitted

There are no anonymous or unverified calls inside Elisence.

Recording, Consent & Compliance

Recording OFF by Default

To protect privacy and reduce risk, Phase 12 treats recording as an exception, not a default:

  • Recording is OFF by default for all calls
  • Any recording feature must be explicitly enabled by ministries
  • Both sides must receive clear, visible consent prompts
  • Storage rules (where, how long, who can access) are governed centrally

Non-Diagnostic Posture

Even when a doctor is on the call, Phase 12 is positioned as:

  • support, coaching and education
  • not a standalone clinical diagnosis engine

This helps keep Elisence on the safe side of regulatory boundaries while allowing value in everyday care.

Accessibility, Voice-First & Languages

Voice-First Experience

Phase 12 works in harmony with the voice and zero-typing engines:

  • Users can start a call using simple voice commands
  • Elisa can assist before and after calls (summaries, next steps)
  • Voice-only calls are prioritised for users with limited bandwidth or comfort

Blind & Low-Vision Support

Combined with accessibility phases, the call UI uses:

  • high-contrast layouts and large controls
  • screen-reader-friendly labels
  • audio cues for join/leave/mute events

Five-Language Foundation

System prompts, call controls and status messages are designed to work across: English, Farsi, Arabic, Turkish and Romanian, with a path for future localisation.

Integration With Other Phases

From Data to Conversation

Phase 12 does not replace other engines — it connects them:

  • Phase 7 & 9: complex Women+ or diabetes journeys can escalate to guided calls when needed.
  • Phase 10: social and profile interactions can safely move into real-time conversation.
  • Phase 11: every call is anchored in the same security passport and audit system.
  • Phase 16 & 17: nutrition and mental health support can include structured, time-boxed sessions.

In practice, Phase 12 gives Elisence a live heartbeat: when data is not enough, people can talk.

Government & Investor View

Governed, Not Chaotic

Unlike generic video platforms, Phase 12 is designed to be configured by ministries:

  • which roles are allowed to call which roles
  • in which regions and for which programmes
  • whether group sessions and webinars are enabled
  • whether recording is allowed and under what policies

This allows GCC governments, hospitals and insurers to deploy real-time care safely inside a controlled ecosystem.

Strategic Value

For investors, Phase 12 is a key part of Elisence’s moat: it turns the platform from a data layer into a living network of real conversations, without losing clinical and regulatory discipline.

In one sentence: Phase 12 gives Elisence a live voice and face — secure voice and video calls that connect citizens, families, professionals and ministries, all governed by the same Zero-Trust, passport-based architecture that protects the rest of the platform.
