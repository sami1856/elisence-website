Zero-Trust Access & Cloudflare Protection

Elisence runs behind a strict Zero-Trust security model. Demo portals, evidence views and future admin consoles are never open to the public – they are wrapped by Cloudflare Access, short-lived sessions, and full audit trails.

This page explains, in plain language for ministries, regulators and partners, how demo.elisence.com and related portals are protected, how invites are issued, and how every visit can be traced and revoked safely.

Zero-Trust Cloudflare Access Invite-Only Demo Short-Lived Sessions Audit Trail Evidence-Grade Security

1. What Zero-Trust Means in Elisence

In Elisence, Zero-Trust means that no part of the system is trusted by default – not the network, not the browser, not even a known IP address. Every sensitive surface must be explicitly allowed, authenticated and logged.

  • No open admin URLs: there are no “secret links” that anyone can guess.
  • No anonymous demo: every serious demo is invite-only, tied to an email identity.
  • Least privilege: guests only see non-diagnostic, synthetic journeys.
  • Full revocation: access can be cut off instantly if needed.

2. Cloudflare Access – How the Demo Is Protected

The public demo at demo.elisence.com sits behind Cloudflare Access. This means that even before the Elisence app sees a request, Cloudflare has already checked the visitor’s identity and session.

  • Email-based invite: guests receive a secure link or one-time code.
  • Session control: access is time-limited; idle sessions expire by design.
  • Device and IP rules: rules can be tightened for ministries or internal pilots.
  • Access logs: every attempt, success or failure is written to an audit trail.

For ministries, this means there is always a clear chain from “who was invited” to “what they saw” to “when they connected.”

3. Invite-Only Evidence & SEAL Packs

While the public website shows a high-level view of Elisence, deeper technical evidence – including SEAL repositories and WORM logs – is never indexed or openly searchable. Instead, it is shared in one of three controlled ways:

  • Invite to demo.elisence.com with pre-configured synthetic journeys.
  • Private GitHub links to SEAL repositories for Women+ and Diabetes.
  • On-request PDF bundles for QDB, QFC, QSTP or ministries under NDA.

This keeps intellectual property safe while still giving reviewers everything they need to verify tests, manifests and SHA-256 anchors.

4. What Ministries and Regulators Can Verify

The Zero-Trust layer is not just a technical choice; it is part of Elisence’s governance story. Ministries and regulators can verify:

  • Who received a demo invite and at which email address.
  • When they logged in and which synthetic journeys were used.
  • That all content stays non-diagnostic and education-only.
  • That no production clinical data is exposed in the demo environment.

Combined with SEAL and WORM evidence, this creates a complete path from architecture → security → behaviour → audit logs.

5. Relationship to Non-Diagnostic & Synthetic Journeys

Zero-Trust in Elisence is tightly linked to two other safety pillars:

  • Non-Diagnostic: even after passing Cloudflare Access, the demo never provides diagnoses, prescriptions or drug dosing advice. It shows education and probabilities only.
  • Synthetic Journeys: all examples in the demo are based on scripted, synthetic scenarios – not real patient data – so there is no risk to citizen privacy.

These three parts – Zero-Trust, Non-Diagnostic, Synthetic – form a consistent safety boundary for every visitor, from investors to ministries.

6. Roadmap: From Demo to National Deployment

As Elisence moves from demo to pilot and then to national deployment, the same Zero-Trust logic scales with it:

  • Citizen access: identity checks and consent flows at the app layer.
  • Clinician and ministry access: Passport V1 roles and Cloudflare policies combined.
  • Multi-region control: rules per country or institution, without changing the core engine.
  • Audit-ready posture: every step remains visible, reviewable and reversible.

This is how Elisence can support Qatar, GCC and other regions as a sovereign, controllable health intelligence layer, not just another app.
