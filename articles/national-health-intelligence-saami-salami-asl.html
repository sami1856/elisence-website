The Future of National Health Intelligence — A Founder Perspective by Saami Salami Asl

By Saami Salami Asl — Founder of Elisence

1. Introduction: Health Systems Are Entering a New Era

Health systems around the world are entering a new era. Prevention, continuity of care, trusted health intelligence, privacy, and safety are becoming central to how modern healthcare platforms should be designed.

From the perspective of Saami Salami Asl, founder of Elisence, the future of national health intelligence must be built around three core principles:

  • Health intelligence that supports people without replacing clinical judgement
  • Privacy-first and Zero-Trust foundations
  • Multi-language, culturally aware, human-centred health support

2. The Core Problem: Fragmentation

Many health systems still rely on fragmented apps, portals, records, databases, and disconnected initiatives. This fragmentation can make health experiences harder for people, families, clinicians, and organisations to navigate.

  • Disconnected information
  • Inconsistent user experience
  • Limited continuity between health journeys
  • Pressure on families and clinicians
  • Reduced ability to support prevention at scale

3. A National Intelligence Layer

Elisence is designed around the idea that national health systems can benefit from a safer, more connected intelligence layer — one that supports prevention, wellbeing, population insight, family health, and long-term continuity without creating unsafe clinical shortcuts.

  • Women+ health support
  • Metabolic and diabetes-related support
  • Nutrition and lifestyle intelligence
  • Environmental and wellbeing context
  • Mental health and calm support
  • Family health and childhood wellbeing

4. Why Safety Matters More Than Ever

National health intelligence must be designed with strong safety, privacy, governance, and explainability from the beginning.

  • Transparent and explainable health intelligence
  • Clear boundaries between support, education, and clinical decision-making
  • Privacy-first system design
  • Governance-aware health platform architecture
  • Zero-Trust foundations for sensitive health contexts

5. The Founder Philosophy: Human First

Technology alone is not enough. The real challenge is designing systems that respect stress, culture, language, privacy, emotion, family life, and human dignity.

Health is human. Health intelligence must therefore be designed to support people in ways that feel clear, calm, respectful, and understandable.

6. Vision for the Next Decade

Over the next decade, health systems are likely to place greater emphasis on prevention, continuity, trusted participation, responsible health intelligence, and culturally aware digital health experiences.

  • Prevention-centred health platforms
  • Trusted health intelligence systems
  • Continuity of care across life stages
  • Personalised nutrition and metabolic support
  • Women-centred and family-aware health strategies

Elisence is being built deliberately for that future — a global health platform designed to support safer, calmer, more connected, and more human health experiences.
