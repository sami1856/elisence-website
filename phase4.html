1. What Phase 4 + GHX Is

Phase 4 is the Global Analytics & Evidence Engine of Elisence. It produces privacy-safe, non-diagnostic, population-level KPIs and insights that can be used by ministries, hospitals, universities, and regulators.

GHX (Governance & Health eXchange) is the interoperability and governance layer that ensures every export is policy-bound, traceable, standards-ready, and audit-proof.

  • Phase 4 answers: “What is happening at population level?”
  • GHX answers: “Can this be exported, integrated, audited, and trusted?”

Both layers operate as non-diagnostic infrastructure: no patient-level diagnosis, no treatment recommendation, and no unsafe exposure of identifiable data.

2. Why It Exists and Why It Matters

Digital health systems fail when evidence is not defensible. Phase 4 + GHX exists to remove doubt: every metric must be reproducible, every comparison must be traceable, and every export must be auditable.

  • For ministries: policy-grade KPIs and public health intelligence.
  • For hospitals: governance-ready safety and outcome monitoring.
  • For universities: research-grade evidence with clear methodology.
  • For regulators: audit logs, provenance, and controlled releases.

This is the moment Elisence becomes health infrastructure, not just an application.

3. BBVP and Zero-Trust Posture

BBVP (Build · Back · Verify · Prove)

  • Build: feature-flagged, clean analytics contracts and deterministic outputs.
  • Back: unit + integration + safety tests for every KPI and rule.
  • Verify: repeatable runs, reproducible outputs, and strict quality gates.
  • Prove: evidence packs, hashes, manifests, and audit references.

Zero-Trust

  • No implicit trust. Inputs are validated. Outputs are gated.
  • No silent changes. Every release is traceable via logs and evidence artifacts.
  • Privacy and safety are enforced by design, not by documentation only.

5. Phase 4 – Global Analytics Engine

Phase 4 computes population-level evidence using strict privacy rules and QA gates. Typical analytics families include:

  • Utilisation and adherence: adoption trends, stability, schedule compliance.
  • Satisfaction and experience: program-level feedback, not individual scoring.
  • Safety signal analytics: MedDRA-coded adverse-event patterns.
  • Effectiveness signals: aggregate outcomes (activated only when data is available).
  • Privacy guards: k-anonymity thresholds and safe aggregation behavior.

No KPI is released without passing quality gates. If safety or privacy thresholds are not met, the engine blocks output rather than risking exposure.

6. GHX – Governance and Interoperability Exchange

GHX turns Phase 4 outputs into integration-ready, policy-bound exports. It is the layer that makes analytics deployable and auditable.

  • Policy Registry: country and region policy packs, versioned and traceable.
  • Consent and access posture: role-bound visibility and export scope controls.
  • FHIR transformation: standards-ready packaging (where applicable).
  • WORM audit trails: append-only logs with reproducible evidence references.
  • Provenance metadata: sources, transforms, QA state, and export lineage.

The result is an exchange layer suitable for ministries and institutions that require documented governance, controlled exports, and standards compatibility.
