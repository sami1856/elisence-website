PHASE 4

Stability, Integrity & Comparative Intelligence Layer

Phase 4 transforms Elisence from an application into a governance-grade, audit-ready health intelligence infrastructure. It is the layer that ministries of health, hospitals, research bodies, and regulators can rely on — because every number, model, and comparison is backed by verifiable integrity, privacy, and scientific rigor.

1. Vision

Phase 4 establishes the foundation where governments, clinicians, and citizens can trust Elisence’s conclusions at national scale. It ensures that:

  • All real-world evidence (RWE) is traceable from input to output
  • Every analytical result is backed by immutable logs and QA
  • Privacy and clinical safety are enforced by design

This is the layer that prepares Elisence for formal evaluation by ministries, national health services, and global partners.

2. Core Engine Architecture

Four pillars: WORM Ledger, FHIR Interoperability, Advanced Provenance, and AKAC.

2.1 WORM Ledger — Immutable Audit Trails

A cryptographically enforced append-only ledger that stores:

  • Dataset fingerprints
  • Model version identifiers
  • QA digests and test outcomes
  • Export hashes and signatures
  • Archive timestamps

Why it matters: Regulators and hospitals can reconstruct exactly how a conclusion was produced. There are no silent edits, no hidden changes, and no trust gaps.

2.2 FHIR Integration — Global Interoperability

Phase 4 harmonises medication and usage data to global medical standards:

  • FHIR resources: Medication, MedicationRequest, MedicationStatement
  • RxNorm / ATC drug identifiers
  • MedDRA for adverse events and side-effect coding
  • SNOMED / ICD-10 for conditions and diagnoses

Why it matters: Ministries, hospitals, and health systems can integrate Elisence without rebuilding their infrastructure. FHIR makes Elisence “plug-into-the-health-system ready”.

2.3 Provenance Engine — End-to-End Traceability

Every analysis, chart, comparison, or export carries embedded provenance metadata:

  • Data sources and ingest paths
  • Transformations and filters applied
  • Model parameters and versions
  • Timestamps, environment, and execution context
  • Digital signatures and QA status
  • AKAC privacy level

This provides true real-world evidence transparency, aligned with UK/EU/US/GCC expectations.

2.4 AKAC — Adaptive Knowledge Access Control

A dynamic privacy supervisor that:

  • Adjusts anonymisation thresholds based on cohort size
  • Applies k-anonymity and safe aggregation rules
  • Blocks unsafe slices, drill-downs, or high-risk combinations
  • Ensures reports cannot deanonymise individuals

Why it matters: Citizens gain confidence that their identity is protected; regulators gain formal proof of compliance; clinicians still receive useful, safe insights.

3. Intelligence & Analytics (Global Medical Intelligence Engine)

Phase 4 houses the analytical brain of Elisence — converting raw usage data into trusted insight for public health, clinical practice, and future decision support.

3.1 Drug Effectiveness Intelligence

The engine computes:

  • Mean effectiveness metrics
  • Response rates across cohorts
  • Δ-change metrics (before/after treatment)
  • Net Benefit Index (NBI)
  • Cohort-specific performance curves

All metrics are validated through QA gates, provenance checks, and population safeguards.

3.2 Side Effect Intelligence

Integrated safety analytics using:

  • MedDRA-coded adverse events
  • Severity weighting and grading
  • PRR / ROR calculations
  • Temporal patterns (onset, duration, recurrence)
  • Geo-demographic risk variations

This produces contextualised safety profiles for public health and clinical support.

3.3 Comparative Intelligence Engine

The flagship capability of Phase 4, enabling:

  • Drug-vs-drug comparisons
  • Cohort matching & propensity weighting
  • Benefit–risk scoring and ranking
  • Multilingual narrative generation (EN/FA/AR/TR/RO)

Narrative example:
“Drug B showed substantially higher effectiveness and fewer severe adverse events than Drug A in this cohort.”

This allows clinicians and ministries to compare therapeutic options without guesswork.

3.4 Global Medical-Intelligence Layer

A unified intelligence fabric that powers:

  • National health reports
  • Population analytics
  • Ministry dashboards and oversight tools
  • Personalised risk–benefit summaries (non-diagnostic)

This layer turns Elisence from an app into a real health system intelligence backbone.

4. Safety, Governance & Compliance

4.1 QA Gates

No analytical output is released unless it passes a chain of QA gates:

  • Completeness and coverage checks
  • Recency and freshness of data
  • Duplicates and outlier detection
  • Internal consistency checks
  • Cohort safety and minimum-size thresholds
  • Privacy and AKAC checks

If any gate fails → the output is blocked, flagged, and not shown.

4.2 Governance of Effectiveness & Adverse Intelligence

  • Transparent, documented methodology
  • Versioned models with full changelog
  • Clear statement of assumptions and limits
  • Clinical-grade metrics and validation
  • Multilingual narratives for global use

4.3 Trust Compliance Framework

Phase 4 aligns with the operational expectations of:

  • GDPR / UK Data Protection Act
  • NHS DTAC principles
  • EU MDR-aligned non-diagnostic AI behaviour
  • HIPAA-style de-identification
  • GCC / Qatar / UAE PDPL requirements
  • ENCePP / STROBE / RECORD-PE for real-world evidence

Outcome: Elisence becomes eligible for formal evaluation by healthcare authorities, ethics boards, and national partners.
