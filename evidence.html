B3 — Ministry Compatibility Evidence (Qatar & GCC Ready)

Elisence is designed from day one as a national-grade digital health platform, not just a consumer app. This section summarises how the platform fits the expectations of ministries of health, public hospitals, QDB/QFC/QSTP evaluators and GCC regulators.

  • Non-diagnostic, non-prescriptive posture: Elisence does not give diagnoses, does not replace doctors, and does not issue prescriptions. It structures data, highlights risk, explains patterns and routes citizens to appropriate care.
  • Built for ministries, hospitals and schools: The platform is designed so that a Ministry of Health, public hospital network, school system or national programme can safely deploy it as part of their own digital health ecosystem — not as a standalone foreign app.
  • Data-sovereignty & hosting: Architecture is compatible with Qatar and wider GCC data-sovereignty expectations: data can be hosted inside the country or approved GCC regions, with clear separation between citizen data, analytics and research exports.
  • Zero-Trust, consent-first, privacy-by-design: All sensitive actions use strict headers, passports and role-based access. Every module follows Zero-Trust principles, with explicit consent, least-privilege access and full audit trails (including WORM logs).
  • Global regulatory alignment (design level): The governance model and evidence pack are aligned with the logic of NHS DCB0129/0160, EU MDR/MDCG, US FDA CDS and GCC data-protection / PDPL frameworks — always within a non-diagnostic decision-support boundary.
  • Automatic country-aware behaviour: Elisence can recognise the user’s country/region and route them to the correct safety rules, disclaimers and policy mappings for that jurisdiction (for example, Qatar vs. UK vs. EU), while keeping one shared technical core.
  • Five-language backbone: Core content and engines are designed around a 5-language baseline — English, Farsi, Arabic, Turkish, Romanian — suitable for GCC, diaspora communities and European partners, with RTL support for Arabic and Farsi.
  • BBVP standard (Build-Back-Verify-Prove): Every phase follows an internal governance standard: engines are built, then verified with tests and WORM logs, and finally proven with manifests and SHA-256 evidence packs before being declared “Done”.

For QDB, QFC, QSTP and ministries, this means Elisence is already operating as a national digital health infrastructure layer, not just a prototype.

Official sector positioning for Qatar & GCC:

  • HealthTech
  • Digital Health
  • AI & Data Intelligence
  • Education / Behaviour Change (EdTech)
  • National Digital Platforms

A dedicated Ministry Compatibility Pack (B3) can be shared privately with Qatar or GCC authorities on request, including policy mappings, data-flow diagrams, safety boundaries and governance documentation.

  • Non-diagnostic posture: Elisence is not a medical device and does not replace doctors. It structures data, flags risk and guides citizens towards care.
  • Usable by ministries & public health systems: Designed for ministries of health, public hospitals, clinics and schools – not only for direct-to-consumer app stores.
  • Data-sovereignty: Architecture designed to respect national data-sovereignty and localisation requirements in Qatar, KSA, UAE and other GCC countries.
  • Five-language backbone: EN / FA / AR / TR / RO – giving a ready base for GCC deployment and wider regions.
  • Governance & Safety: Zero-Trust model, WORM audit, consent-first flows and privacy-by-design as core principles.

A deeper ministry-focused Evidence Pack can include detailed policy mappings, data-flow diagrams and governance documents, shared privately with ministries and regulators in Qatar and the wider GCC.
